Which hormone is responsible for the development of secondary sexual traits in males?

(A) Estrogen

(B) Epinephrine

(C) Progesterone

(D) ** Testosterone

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Testosterone is also involved in regulating secondary male characteristics, which are those responsible for masculinity.

Concept note-2: -Estrogen and progesterone are the sex hormones in females. Estrogen is responsible for the development of secondary sexual characters in females and progesterone is required to maintain pregnancy and delivery.

Concept note-3: -Testosterone plays a key role in the development of male reproductive tissues such as testes and prostate. They are also responsible for the development of secondary sexual characters such as the growth of facial/ pubic/ body hair, deepening of the voice, increased muscle or bone mass.

Concept note-4: -Testosterone is a sex hormone that plays important roles in the body. A small amount of circulating testosterone is converted to estradiol, a form of estrogen.
