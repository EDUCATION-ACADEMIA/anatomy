How was my quiz

(A) ** Awesome

(B) ** Good

(C) ** Nice

(D) ** Bad

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Is it normal to cry over a bad grade? It’s ok to cry, it really is. The stress of preparing for a test is naturally a strain on many levels including emotionally and getting a bad grade does not mean anything more than that you don’t understand the subject well enough to test high on it.

Concept note-2: -Don’t Panic. If you’ve always done well in school-or even if you haven’t-a failing grade can come as a shock. Carefully Review Your Exam. When I failed my chemistry exam, I barely looked at the test. Make a Plan. Go to Office Hours. Prepare for the Next Exam.

Concept note-3: -Inhibit high level cognitive and evaluative skills. The quiz might be testing a user’s logical and interpretative skills rather than knowledge acquired. Can restrict insights into how complete an understanding a learner has gained due to the element of chance involved.

Concept note-4: -Step 1: Move Forward. It is quite easy to get caught up in your bad exam mark, and letting it consume your emotions. Step 2: Work Harder. If you get a bad exam mark, you need to come back ten times harder. Step 3: Take Some Constructive Criticism.
