What is the Parathyroid?

(A) None of the above

(B) Located in the chest area and releases hormones which stimulate T cell development

(C) ** Gland that releases hormones which regulate the level of calcium in the blood

(D) Located in the brain and regulated the other glands in the endocrine system

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -These glands, located behind the thyroid at the bottom of your neck, are about the size of a grain of rice. The parathyroid hormone produced by the thyroid glands helps maintain the right balance of calcium in the bloodstream and in tissues that depend on calcium for proper functioning.

Concept note-2: -Parathyroid hormone (PTH) is a hormone that your parathyroid glands make and release to control the level of calcium in your blood by increasing it. PTH also helps control the levels of phosphorus (a mineral) and vitamin D (a hormone) in your blood and bones.

Concept note-3: -Parathyroid glands produce parathyroid hormone, which plays a key role in the regulation of calcium levels in the blood. Precise calcium levels are important in the human body, since small changes can cause muscle and nerve problems.

Concept note-4: -Parathyroid hormone regulates calcium levels in the blood, largely by increasing the levels when they are too low. It does this through its actions on the kidneys, bones and intestine: Bones – parathyroid hormone stimulates the release of calcium from large calcium stores in the bones into the bloodstream.

Concept note-5: -Also called parathormone, parathyrin, and PTH.
