Adrenocorticotrophic hormone (ACTH) is produced by the ____ and targets the ____

(A) ** pituitary, adrenal

(B) adrenal, hypothalamus

(C) adrenal, pituitary

(D) adrenal, kidneys

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Corticotropin-releasing hormone (CRH ) is released from the hypothalamus, which stimulates the anterior pituitary to release adrenocorticotropic hormone (ACTH). ACTH then acts on its target organ, the adrenal cortex .

Concept note-2: -ACTH is a hormone made by the pituitary gland, a small gland at the base of the brain. ACTH controls the production of another hormone called cortisol. Cortisol is made by the adrenal glands, two small glands located above the kidneys.

Concept note-3: -A hormone made in the pituitary gland. Adrenocorticotropic hormone acts on the outer part of the adrenal gland to control its release of corticosteroid hormones. More adrenocorticotropic hormone is made during times of stress.

Concept note-4: -The anterior lobe of your pituitary gland makes and releases the following hormones: Adrenocorticotropic hormone (ACTH or corticotrophin): ACTH plays a role in how your body responds to stress.
