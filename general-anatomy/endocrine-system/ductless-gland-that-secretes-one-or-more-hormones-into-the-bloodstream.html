GENERAL ANATOMY

ENDOCRINE SYSTEM

DOWNLOAD FREE PDF BOOK (USEFUL FOR TEACHERS/INSTRUCTORS/COACHING INSTITUTES)

Ductless gland that secretes one or more hormones into the bloodstream.

(A) endocrine system

(B) exocrine system

(C) adrenal glands

(D) ** endocrine gland

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Endocrine glands are ductless glands of the endocrine system that secrete their products, hormones, directly into the blood. The major glands of the endocrine system include the pineal gland, pituitary gland, pancreas, ovaries, testes, thyroid gland, parathyroid gland, hypothalamus and adrenal glands.

Concept note-2: -Endocrine glands are ductless glands and release the substances that they make (hormones) directly into the bloodstream.

Concept note-3: -Endocrine glands are also known as the ductless glands because of the fact that their secretions are released directly into the blood, not to any tubes or ducts.

Concept note-4: -The endocrine glands do not have ducts to carry their product to a surface. They are called ductless glands.

Concept note-5: -Compare and contrast endocrine and exocrine glands. Endocrine glands are ductless. They release their secretion into the surrounding fluid, from which it enters the bloodstream or lymph to travel to distant cells.
