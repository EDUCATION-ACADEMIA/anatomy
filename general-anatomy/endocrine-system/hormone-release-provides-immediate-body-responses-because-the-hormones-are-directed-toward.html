Hormone release provides immediate body responses because the hormones are directed toward:

(A) ** target organ cells

(B) cardiac cells

(C) nerve cells

(D) synaptic junctions

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -So why do hormones affect only their target cells in particular tissues? Because only those target cells have receptors for that particular hormone. Some hormones bind to receptors on the surface of target cells. Others enter the cells and bind to receptors in the cytoplasm or nucleus.

Concept note-2: -Paracrine, autocrine and synaptic are three types of local hormone signaling. In paracrine signaling, hormones are released into the fluid between cells (the interstitial fluid) and diffuse to nearby target cells.

Concept note-3: -Endocrine glands release hormones into the bloodstream. This lets the hormones travel to cells in other parts of the body.
