A goiter is an enlargement of what organ?

(A) pancreas

(B) liver

(C) ** thyroid

(D) hypothalamus

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A goiter (GOI-tur) is the irregular growth of the thyroid gland. The thyroid is a butterfly-shaped gland located at the base of the neck just below the Adam’s apple.

Concept note-2: -Definition. A simple goiter is an enlargement of the thyroid gland. It is usually not a tumor or cancer.

Concept note-3: -A goiter is an enlargement of the thyroid, the H-shaped gland that wraps around the front of your windpipe, just below your Adam’s apple.

Concept note-4: -Hashimoto’s thyroiditis is a more common cause of goiter formation in the US. This is an autoimmune condition in which there is destruction of the thyroid gland by one’s own immune system. As the gland becomes more damaged, it is less able to make adequate supplies of thyroid hormone.

Concept note-5: -A goiter is used to describe any enlarged thyroid gland. The thyroid is a butterfly-shaped gland located in your neck.
