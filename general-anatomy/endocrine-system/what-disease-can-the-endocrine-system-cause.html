What disease can the endocrine system cause?

(A) endosis

(B) ** osteoporosis

(C) hormonetopia

(D) pneumonoultramicroscopicsilicovolcanoconiosis

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Disorders of the endocrine system, such as primary hyperparathyroidism, hyperthyroidism, hypogonadism, growth hormone deficiency, Cushing’s syndrome, and anorexia nervosa frequently cause secondary osteoporosis.

Concept note-2: -Disorders of the endocrine system, such as hyperthyroidism, hypogonadism, growth hormone deficiency, and Cushing’s syndrome frequently cause secondary osteoporosis.

Concept note-3: -January 24, 2022. Osteoporosis refers to weak bones. Osteopenia is another term, sometimes used to describe a less severe condition of weaker bones. Both are “silent” diseases because bone loss often occurs without you knowing it. As a result, bones become weak, and are more likely to break (fracture).

Concept note-4: -The majority of postmenopausal women with osteoporosis have bone loss related to estrogen deficiency. The rapid bone loss results from an increase in bone turnover with an imbalance between bone resorption and bone formation.

Concept note-5: -A decrease in estrogen in women at the time of menopause and a decrease in testosterone in men as they age.
