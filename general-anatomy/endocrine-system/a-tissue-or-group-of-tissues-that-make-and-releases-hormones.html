A tissue or group of tissues that make and releases hormones.

(A) Endocrine System

(B) Pancreas

(C) Flight or Fight Response

(D) ** Glands

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Refers to tissue that makes and releases hormones that travel in the bloodstream and control the actions of other cells or organs. Some examples of endocrine tissues are the pituitary, thyroid, and adrenal glands.

Concept note-2: -The endocrine tissue is a complex of various glands that produce hormones involved in bodily processes such as metabolic processes, water and electrolyte balance, maturation, growth and reproduction.

Concept note-3: -The endocrine system is made up of glands that make hormones. Hormones are the bodyâ€™s chemical messengers. They carry information and instructions from one set of cells to another. The endocrine (pronounced: EN-duh-krin) system influences almost every cell, organ, and function of our bodies.

Concept note-4: -The endocrine system consists of: Glands located throughout the body; Hormones made by the glands and released into the bloodstream or the fluid surrounding cells; and. Receptors in various organs and tissues that recognize and respond to the hormones.
