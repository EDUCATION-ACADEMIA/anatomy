Chewing involves voluntary muscles-these are ones that we consciously control-but once we swallow, digestion becomes involuntary.Which sentence from the section provides further support for the claim?

(A) Our teeth start the process by chewing and grinding up each mouthful of food.

(B) The tongue works it into a ball-shaped mass, or bolus, for swallowing.

(C) Saliva uses specialized proteins called enzymes.

(D) ** It is sealed in the stomach by two ringlike sphincter muscles for several hours.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Digestion involves mixing food with digestive juices, moving it through the digestive tract, and breaking down large molecules of food into smaller molecules. Digestion begins in the mouth, when you chew and swallow, and is completed in the small intestine.

Concept note-2: -The digestive system breaks down the food we eat into tiny parts to give us fuel and the nutrients we need to live.

Concept note-3: -Peristalsis Is the Contraction of Muscle Tissue That Helps Move and Break Down Foodstuffs. The walls of the alimentary canal include layers of smooth muscle controlled by the autonomic nervous system. Alternating contraction and relaxation of these muscles is called peristalsis.

Concept note-4: -Mouth. The digestive process starts in your mouth when you chew. Your salivary glands make saliva, a digestive juice, which moistens food so it moves more easily through your esophagus into your stomach. Saliva also has an enzyme that begins to break down starches in your food.
