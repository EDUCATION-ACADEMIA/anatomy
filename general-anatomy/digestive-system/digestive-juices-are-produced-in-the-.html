Digestive juices are produced in the ____

(A) mouth, stomach

(B) stomach, small intestine

(C) ** mouth, stomach, small intestine

(D) mouth, stomach, large intestine

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Your small intestine makes digestive juice, which mixes with bile and pancreatic juice to complete the breakdown of proteins, carbohydrates, and fats. Bacteria in your small intestine make some of the enzymes you need to digest carbohydrates.

Concept note-2: -Digestive juice produced by the small intestine combines with pancreatic juice and bile to complete digestion. The body completes the breakdown of proteins, and the final breakdown of starches produces glucose molecules that absorb into the blood.

Concept note-3: -Saliva contains special enzymes that help digest the starches in your food. An enzyme called amylase breaks down starches (complex carbohydrates) into sugars, which your body can more easily absorb. Saliva also contains an enzyme called lingual lipase, which breaks down fats.

Concept note-4: -Solution : The digestive juice secreted by the stomach is the gastric juice. (c) Enzyme.

Concept note-5: -Secretions. The abomasum is the only stomach compartment with glandular mucosa that can secrete digestive juices, including hydrochloric acid, pepsin, and rennin.
