Which organ makes bile, detoxifies blood, and filters out dead red blood cells?

(A) ** liver

(B) gall bladder

(C) pancreas

(D) large intestines

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -More than 500 vital functions have been identified with the liver. Some of the more well-known functions include the following: Production of bile, which helps carry away waste and break down fats in the small intestine during digestion. Production of certain proteins for blood plasma.

Concept note-2: -The liver is well known to metabolize and excrete into bile many compounds and toxins, thus eliminating them (usually) from the body.

Concept note-3: -Together with the spleen, the liver helps to degrade old red blood cells into breakdown products, such as bilirubin and other bile pigments. The liver extracts these products from the blood for elimination via urine and stool.

Concept note-4: -Bile is the greenish-yellow fluid (consisting of waste products, cholesterol, and bile salts) that is secreted by the liver cells to perform 2 primary functions: To carry away waste. To break down fats during digestion.

Concept note-5: -The liver is the largest solid organ in the body. It removes toxins from the bodyâ€™s blood supply, maintains healthy blood sugar levels, regulates blood clotting, and performs hundreds of other vital functions. It is located beneath the rib cage in the right upper abdomen.
