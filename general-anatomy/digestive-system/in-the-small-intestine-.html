GENERAL ANATOMY

DIGESTIVE SYSTEM

DOWNLOAD FREE PDF BOOK (USEFUL FOR TEACHERS/INSTRUCTORS/COACHING INSTITUTES)

In the small intestine ____

(A) waste products pass through until they form solid faeces

(B) water is added to soften the food

(C) ** digested food is absorbed into the blood

(D) waste products leave the body

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The muscles of the small intestine mix food with digestive juices from the pancreas, liver, and intestine, and push the mixture forward for further digestion. The walls of the small intestine absorb water and the digested nutrients into your bloodstream.

Concept note-2: -The middle part of your small intestine is the jejunum. The jejunum absorbs most of your nutrients: carbohydrates, fats, minerals, proteins, and vitamins. The lowest part of your small intestine is the ileum. This is where the final parts of digestive absorption take place.

Concept note-3: -The small intestine absorbs most digested food molecules, as well as water and minerals, and passes them on to other parts of the body for storage or further chemical change. Specialized cells help absorbed materials cross the intestinal lining into the bloodstream.

Concept note-4: -Villi That Line the Walls of the Small Intestine Absorb Nutrients. Villi that line the walls of the small intestine absorb nutrients into capillaries of the circulatory system and lacteals of the lymphatic system. Villi contain capillary beds, as well as lymphatic vessels called lacteals.

Concept note-5: -The digested food is absorbed inside the small intestine which have finger like projections called villi in its inner wall. These villi comprise networks of fine blood capillaries close to their surface. The food from the villi passes into these capillaries thereby getting absorbed into the blood.
