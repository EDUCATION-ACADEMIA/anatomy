Peristalsis refers to

(A) one of the digestive organs

(B) the action of the stomach

(C) heart rate increasing

(D) ** smooth muscles moving food through the digestive system

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Peristalsis is a series of wave-like muscle contractions that move food through the digestive tract. It starts in the esophagus where strong wave-like motions of the smooth muscle move balls of swallowed food to the stomach.

Concept note-2: -The muscle movements of peristalsis are often described as wave-like because they contract and relax in a continuous pattern to move food forward. They involve both the circular muscles that ring the tubes of your digestive tract and also longitudinal muscles that span the walls of the tubes.

Concept note-3: -Visceral smooth muscle arranged in layers around a hollow organ generally produces slow, steady contractions known as peristalsis that allow substances, such as food in the digestive tract, to move through the body.

Concept note-4: -Peristalsis: The movement of food in the alimentary canal is mediated by the process called peristalsis. It is a type of involuntary muscular movement that occurs in the digestive system. It begins with the swallowing of food in the mouth and propulsion throughout the gastrointestinal tract.

Concept note-5: -The peristaltic movement, also known as peristalsis, is the contraction and relaxation of the oesophagus and the food pipe, which causes the food to be pushed down the track to the stomach. This involuntary movement is required to transport food through the stomach and bowels through the anus.
