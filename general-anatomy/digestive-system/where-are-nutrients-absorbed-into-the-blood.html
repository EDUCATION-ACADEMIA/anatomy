Where are nutrients absorbed into the blood?

(A) esophagus

(B) ** small intestine

(C) stomach

(D) mouth

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The walls of the small intestine absorb water and the digested nutrients into your bloodstream.

Concept note-2: -Ileum: This last section is the longest part of your small intestine. The ileum is where most of the nutrients from your food are absorbed before emptying into the large intestine.

Concept note-3: -Small intestine. The walls of the small intestine absorb the digested nutrients into the bloodstream. The blood delivers the nutrients to the rest of the body.

Concept note-4: -The food that is digested is absorbed into the blood vessels in the walls of the intestine. The finger-like projections known as villi, drastically increase the surface area of the small intestine for greater absorption of the digested food. The blood carries the absorbed food material to different parts of the body.

Concept note-5: -Glucose, fructose, galactose and amino acid are absorbed into blood and fatty acid and glycerol are absorbed into the lymph of lacteal.
