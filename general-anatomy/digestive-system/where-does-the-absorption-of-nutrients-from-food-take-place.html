Where does the absorption of nutrients from food take place?

(A) stomach

(B) liver

(C) ** small intestine

(D) large intestine

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The middle part of your small intestine is the jejunum. The jejunum absorbs most of your nutrients: carbohydrates, fats, minerals, proteins, and vitamins. The lowest part of your small intestine is the ileum. This is where the final parts of digestive absorption take place.

Concept note-2: -In the small intestine, absorption occurs through the lumenal face of the mucosa, which is covered with finger-like projections called villi. Each villus is bordered by several pouches or invaginations called crypts. On the average, each villus is surrounded by eight crypts.

Concept note-3: -The small intestine has three parts: the duodenum, jejunum, and ileum. It helps to further digest food coming from the stomach. It absorbs nutrients (vitamins, minerals, carbohydrates, fats, proteins) and water from food so they can be used by the body.

Concept note-4: -The presence of small finger-like projections called villi in the duodenum aids in food absorption. The jejunum has an enterocyte cell lining that digests small nutrients with the help of digestive enzymes. The ileum aids in the absorption of bile salts, vitamin B12, and other byproducts.
