The organs of the digestive system can be divided into two categories, primary and secondary. Which category has a direct role in digesting food and comes into physical contact with the food?

(A) ** Primary Organs

(B) Secondary Organs

(C) Both

(D) Neither

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Regions of the digestive system can be divided into two main parts: the alimentary tract and accessory organs. The alimentary tract of the digestive system is composed of the mouth, pharynx, esophagus, stomach, small and large intestines, rectum and anus.

Concept note-2: -The gastrointestinal tract consists of the oral cavity, pharynx, esophagus, stomach, small intestine, and large intestine. The accessory organs are the teeth, tongue, and glandular organs such as salivary glands, liver, gallbladder, and pancreas.

Concept note-3: -Answer and Explanation: Two organs of the digestive system that work in unison are the pancreas and small intestine. The pancreas, an endocrine organ and gland, is known to secrete pancreatic juices that can break down food and extract some nutrients.

Concept note-4: -Answer and Explanation: The following organs come in contact with food: mouth, esophagus, stomach, small and large intestine, rectum, and anus.
