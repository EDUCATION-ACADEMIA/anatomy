GENERAL ANATOMY

DIGESTIVE SYSTEM

[SOURCES]
What have you learn about the digestive system?

(A) ** breaks down food and gives you energy

(B) Allows you to breath and gives you energy

(C) Gives you nutrients and breaks down food

(D) None

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The digestive system converts the foods we eat into their simplest forms, like glucose (sugars), amino acids (that make up protein) or fatty acids (that make up fats). The broken-down food is then absorbed into the bloodstream from the small intestine and the nutrients are carried to each cell in the body.

Concept note-2: -Your digestive system takes in food, breaks it down into the nutrients and energy your body needs, and then gets rid of the waste. Most of your digestive system is a long tube that runs from your mouth to your rectum. This “tube” includes your esophagus, stomach, and intestines.

Concept note-3: -Why is Digestive Health Important? The digestive system plays a major role in your overall health as it brings nutrients to your body. Without good digestive health, you will experience a host of uncomfortable symptoms such as abdominal pain, bloating, indigestion, and more.

Concept note-4: -The pancreas makes enzymes that help digest proteins, fats, and carbs. It also makes a substance that neutralizes stomach acid. These enzymes and bile travel through special pathways (called ducts) into the small intestine, where they help to break down food. The liver also helps process nutrients in the bloodstream.
