Which two organs does both mechanical and chemical digestion occur?

(A) Mouth and Large Intestine

(B) Mouth and Esophagus

(C) Stomach and Small Intestine

(D) ** Stomach and Mouth

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Proper digestion requires both mechanical and chemical digestion and occurs in the oral cavity, stomach, and small intestine.

Concept note-2: -Most mechanical and some chemical digestion occur in the stomach. Mechanical digestion occurs as layers of muscle in the stomach lining contract to produce a mixing and churning motion. Chemical digestion is carried out by stomach juices that contain enzymes and hydrochloric acid.

Concept note-3: -Mechanical digestion begins in your mouth with chewing, then moves to churning in the stomach and segmentation in the small intestine. Peristalsis is also part of mechanical digestion.

Concept note-4: -The main chemical digestion processes in the stomach occurs through the action of gastric juices. Gastric juice contains the enzyme pepsin (secreted as pepsinogen), which hydrolyses the chemical breakdown of proteins.

Concept note-5: -The main organs that make up the digestive system (in order of their function) are the mouth, esophagus, stomach, small intestine, large intestine, rectum and anus.
