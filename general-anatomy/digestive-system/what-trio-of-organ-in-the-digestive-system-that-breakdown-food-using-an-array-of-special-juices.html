DIGESTIVE SYSTEM

what trio of organ in the digestive system that breakdown food using an array of special juices?

(A) ** liver, gallbladder, pancreas

(B) esophagus, stomach, colon

(C) brain, heart, bladder

(D) none of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The muscles of the small intestine mix food with digestive juices from the pancreas, liver, and intestine, and push the mixture forward for further digestion. The walls of the small intestine absorb water and the digested nutrients into your bloodstream.

Concept note-2: -The liver, gallbladder, and pancreas are a trio of organs that break down food using a array of special juices.Th bodyâ€™s enzymes, hormones, nerves, and blood; all work together to break down food, modulate the digestive process, and deliver its final products.

Concept note-3: -To help break food down, the small intestine receives digestive juices from other organs in your digestive system, including your liver, gallbladder and pancreas. Ducts from these organs feed into the duodenum.

Concept note-4: -Your digestive system is made up of the gastrointestinal (GI) tract and your liver, pancreas and gallbladder.

Concept note-5: -The digestive role of the liver is to produce bile and export it to the duodenum. The gallbladder primarily stores, concentrates, and releases bile. The pancreas produces pancreatic juice, which contains digestive enzymes and bicarbonate ions, and delivers it to the duodenum.
