Which of the following is an accessory organ?

(A) Stomach

(B) Small Intesting

(C) ** Liver

(D) Large Intestine

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The accessory digestive organs are the tongue, salivary glands, pancreas, liver, and gallbladder.

Concept note-2: -The accessory organs are the teeth, tongue, and glandular organs such as salivary glands, liver, gallbladder, and pancreas.

Concept note-3: -Bile contains waste products, making the liver an organ of excretion. Bile also has an important role in digestion, making the liver an accessory organ of digestion.

Concept note-4: -Accessory organs are organs which are not part of the digestive system; however, they aid in the digestion process by performing many secondary functions. Pancreas, gall bladder and liver are the three accessory organs in digestive system.

Concept note-5: -The accessory organs include the liver, gallbladder, and pancreas. These organs secrete or store substances that are carried to the duodenum of the small intestine as needed for digestion.
