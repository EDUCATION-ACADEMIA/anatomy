Where is the bile produced?

(A) ** Liver

(B) Pancreas

(C) Salivary glands

(D) Gall bladder

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Bile is a fluid that is made and released by the liver and stored in the gallbladder. Bile helps with digestion. It breaks down fats into fatty acids, which can be taken into the body by the digestive tract.

Concept note-2: -Bile is produced in your liver and stored in your gallbladder. Eating a meal that contains even a small amount of fat signals your gallbladder to release bile, which flows through a small tube into the upper part of your small intestine (duodenum).

Concept note-3: -Bile is made in the liver and flows through the common hepatic duct and the cystic duct to the gallbladder, where it is stored. When food is being digested, bile is released from the gallbladder and flows through the common bile duct and pancreas into small intestine.

Concept note-4: -Bile is formed initially at the level of the hepatocytes by the net movement of water and solutes into the bile canaliculi. Bile secretory pressure normally exceeds he-patic perfusion pressure, both in the isolated perfused liver (15-17) and in uiuo (18, 19).
