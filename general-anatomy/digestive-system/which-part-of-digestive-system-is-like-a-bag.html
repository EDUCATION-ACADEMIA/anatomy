Which part of digestive system is like a bag?

(A) Food pipe

(B) Intestine

(C) ** Stomach

(D) Tongue

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The gallbladder is a small pouch that stores bile. The gallbladder release bile into the duodenum to help digest fats in the food you eat.

Concept note-2: -The stomach is a J-shaped organ in the upper belly (abdomen). It’s part of the digestive system. It’s between the end of the food pipe (esophagus) and the start of the first part of the small bowel (duodenum). The stomach is much like a bag with a lining.

Concept note-3: -Stomach is the bag-like structure that receives food from the food pipe.

Concept note-4: -This structure provides maximum surface area so that proper digestion of food takes place. The tube-like structure facilitates the proper movement of the food and the bag-like structure enables the better digestion of food.

Concept note-5: -Stomach: An organ with strong muscular walls, the stomach holds the food and mixes it with acid and enzymes that continue to break the food down into a liquid or paste. Small Intestine (Small Bowel): Almost 20 feet long, the small intestine is the workhorse of the digestive system.
