What does the stomach do to your food?

(A) Pulls the vitamins and minerals out.

(B) Helps move food to the back of your throat.

(C) ** Turns it into a liquid mixture.

(D) Collects the waste.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The stomach muscles churn and mix the food with digestive juices that have acids and enzymes. These juices help break up food into much smaller pieces. By the time food is ready to leave the stomach, it has been turned into a thick liquid called chyme (kime).

Concept note-2: -Stomach: An organ with strong muscular walls, the stomach holds the food and mixes it with acid and enzymes that continue to break the food down into a liquid or paste.

Concept note-3: -This is where digestive juices and enzymes break down the food that you chewed and swallowed. It prepares it to provide your body with energy.
