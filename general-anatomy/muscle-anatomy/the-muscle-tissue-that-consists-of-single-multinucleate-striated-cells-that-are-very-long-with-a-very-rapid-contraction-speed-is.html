The muscle tissue that consists of single, multinucleate, striated cells that are very long with a very rapid contraction speed is:

(A) cardiac muscle only

(B) ** skeletal muscle only

(C) cardiac and skeletal muscle

(D) smooth muscle only

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Skeletal muscle fibers are cylindrical, multinucleated, striated, and under voluntary control.

Concept note-2: -Skeletal muscle fibers occur in muscles which are attached to the skeleton. They are striated in appearance and are under voluntary control.

Concept note-3: -Skeletal muscle cells are long, cylindrical, and striated. They are multi-nucleated meaning that they have more than one nucleus.

Concept note-4: -The cardiac muscle cell has one central nucleus, like smooth muscle, but it also is striated, like skeletal muscle.
