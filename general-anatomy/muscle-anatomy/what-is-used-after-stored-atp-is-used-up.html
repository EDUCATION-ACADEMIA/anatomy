What is used after stored ATP is used up?

(A) anaerobic respiration

(B) ** creatine phosphate

(C) aerobic respiration

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Once the available ATP is used up, which occurs in a few seconds, a molecule called phosphocreatine is used to re-form ATP in the muscle.

Concept note-2: -In the process of regeneration of ATP, creatine phosphate transfers a high-energy phosphate to ADP. The products of this reaction are ATP and creatine.

Concept note-3: -Creatine-P helps maintain a constant concentration of ATP in muscle during sudden bursts or exercise that would otherwise deplete ATP concentration in the cell.

Concept note-4: -Abstract. THE hydrolysis of ATP provides the energy source for muscular contraction. ATP is either stored in the muscle or produced by phosphorylation of ADP by creatine phosphate or glycolytic processes, or through oxidative processes in the mitochondria.
