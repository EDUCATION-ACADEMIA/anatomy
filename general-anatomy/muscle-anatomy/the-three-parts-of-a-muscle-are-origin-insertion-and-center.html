The three parts of a muscle are origin, insertion, and center.

(A) True

(B) ** False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The three parts of a muscle are origin, insertion, and center. False-The three parts of a muscle are origin, insertion, and belly. The chief motor nerve of the face is the fifth cranial nerve.

Concept note-2: -Perimysium: The middle layer surrounding bundles of muscle fibers. Endomysium: The innermost layer surrounding individual muscle fibers.

Concept note-3: -What is true of the origin part of a muscle? It is attached to the skeleton.

Concept note-4: -A skeletal muscle attaches to bone (or sometimes other muscles or tissues) at two or more places. If the place is a bone that remains immobile for an action, the attachment is called an origin. If the place is on the bone that moves during the action, the attachment is called an insertion.

Concept note-5: -The origin of a muscle is the end of the muscle attached to an immovable part. The insertion is the end of a muscle attached to a moveable part. When a muscle contracts, the insertion is pulled toward the origin.
