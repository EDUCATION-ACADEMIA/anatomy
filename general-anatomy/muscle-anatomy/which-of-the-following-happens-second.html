Which of the following happens second?

(A) Muscle fiber is wrapped in endomysium

(B) ** Multiple fibers are bundled together in a fascicle and wrapped in perimysium

(C) Multiple fasicles are wrapped together in epimysium

(D) The epimysium extends to form an aponeurosis or a tendon

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A muscle fascicle is a bundle of skeletal muscle fibers surrounded by perimysium, a type of connective tissue.

Concept note-2: -Each bundle of muscle fiber is called a fasciculus and is surrounded by a layer of connective tissue called the perimysium. Within the fasciculus, each individual muscle cell, called a muscle fiber, is surrounded by connective tissue called the endomysium.

Concept note-3: -Each organized skeletal muscle in human body is made of a number of muscle bundles or fascicles held together by a common collagenous connective tissue layer called fascia.

Concept note-4: -Muscle Fascia Within this is a layer termed the epimysium which splits inwards into the muscle as the perimysium dividing muscle fibers into groups termed fascicle. Each fascicle is surrounded by another layer of connective tissue termed the endomysium.
