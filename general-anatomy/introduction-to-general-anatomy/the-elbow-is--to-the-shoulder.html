INTRODUCTION TO GENERAL ANATOMY

[SOURCES]
The elbow is ____ to the shoulder.

(A) Proximal

(B) ** Distal

(C) Anterior

(D) Lateral

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Proximal means ‘closer to the origin of a structure’, like a limb, while distal means ‘farther away from the structure’s origin’. Thus, when comparing the shoulder to the elbow, the shoulder is proximal and the elbow is distal.

Concept note-2: -Distal: farther away from a point of reference or attachment (ex: the elbow is distal to the shoulder or the wrist is distal to the elbow.

Concept note-3: -In medicine, it refers to parts of the body further away from the center. For example, the hand is distal to the shoulder.

Concept note-4: -Answer and Explanation: The anatomical direction of the shoulder to the elbow is proximal.

Concept note-5: -The shoulder is considered superior (above) and proximal to the elbow, indicating that it is closer to the point of attachment.
