during digestion, what is the effect of mechanical breakdown on chemical breakdown?

(A) mechanical breakdown has no effect on chemical breakdown

(B) mechanical breakdown slows down chemical breakdown

(C) sometimes mechanical will speed up chemical and some times it will slow it down

(D) ** mechanical breakdown always speeds up the process of chemical breakdown

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Mechanical digestion involves physically breaking down food substances into smaller particles to more efficiently undergo chemical digestion. The role of chemical digestion is to further degrade the molecular structure of the ingested compounds by digestive enzymes into a form that is absorbable into the bloodstream.

Concept note-2: -The mechanical breakdown occurs in the mouth during mastication (chewing action) by the teeth. This prepares the ingested food for further chemical digestion in the mouth and the stomach by enzymes. Chemical digestion starts at the mouth where the enzymes such as amylase break down the starches from the food.

Concept note-3: -Mechanical digestion begins in your mouth with chewing, then moves to churning in the stomach and segmentation in the small intestine.

Concept note-4: -Mechanical digestion is a purely physical process that does not change the chemical nature of the food. Instead, it makes the food smaller to increase both surface area and mobility. It includes mastication, or chewing, as well as tongue movements that help break food into smaller bits and mix food with saliva.

Concept note-5: -Mechanical digestion can only break up the food particles into smaller pieces. A chemical digestion process called enzymatic hydrolysis can break the bonds holding the molecular ‘building blocks’ within the food together.
