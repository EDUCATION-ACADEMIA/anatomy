Tendons connect ____ to ____

(A) ** Bone to Muscle

(B) Bone to Bone

(C) Muscle to Muscle

(D) Muscle to Skin

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Tough, fibrous, cord-like tissue that connects muscle to bone or another structure, such as an eyeball. Tendons help the bone or structure to move.

Concept note-2: -The tendon is attached to the bone by collagenous fibres (Sharpey fibres) that continue into the matrix of the bone.

Concept note-3: -Myotendinous junction. The junction that connects muscles to tendon cells. It is composed of ECM components, secreted into the space between the two cell types, that bind to integrin receptors on these cells.

Concept note-4: -Skeletal muscle is attached by cord-like tendons to bone, such as in the legs, arms, and face. Skeletal muscles are called striated (pronounced: STRY-ay-ted) because they are made up of fibers that have horizontal stripes when viewed under a microscope.

Concept note-5: -The tissue that attaches bones to bones is called a ligament and the tissue that attaches muscles to bones is called a tendon.
