(B) y =-(x-7)2-10

(C) y =-(x-7)2 + 10

(D) ** y =-(x + 7)2-10

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The vertex form of a quadratic function is f(x) = a(x – h)2 + k, where a, h, and k are constants. of the parabola is at (h, k). When the quadratic parent function f(x) = x2 is written in vertex form, y = a(x – h)2 + k, a = 1, h = 0, and k = 0.

Concept note-2: -1 Answer. The vertex form is: y−yv=a(x−xv)2 .

Concept note-3: -∴, the vertex form is y=(x−3)2−4 .

Concept note-4: -Vertex form of a quadratic equation is y=a(x-h)2+k, where (h, k) is the vertex of the parabola. The vertex of a parabola is the point at the top or bottom of the parabola. ’h’ is-6, the first coordinate in the vertex. ’k’ is-4, the second coordinate in the vertex. ’x’ is-2, the first coordinate in the other point.
