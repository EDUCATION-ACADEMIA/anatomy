chewing surface of posterior teeth

(A) ** occlusion

(B) mesial

(C) bite

(D) functionalism

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Occlusal– The chewing surface of posterior teeth.

Concept note-2: -Occlusal: It’s the chewing surface of posterior teeth. Proximal: It’s the region in tooth surfaces that are next to each other i.e., distal of the lateral incisor and mesial of canine.

Concept note-3: -Occlusal – The chewing surface of the tooth. Mesial – The forward side of the tooth. The mesial of the tooth is found on the “in between surface” of the tooth next to it. Distal – The back side of the tooth.

Concept note-4: -The five tooth surfaces are occlusal (top of the tooth), mesial (front of the tooth), distal (back of the tooth), facial (side of the tooth facing the cheek), and lingual (side of the tooth facing the tongue).

Concept note-5: -Cross Bite. Open Bite. Overbite. Underbite.
