What is the difference between anatomy and physiology?

(A) One starts with A and the other with an P.

(B) ** Anatomy is study of shape and structure, and Physiology is study of functions.

(C) Physiology is study of shape and structure, and Anatomy is study of functions.

(D) None are correct.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Anatomy and physiology are two of the most basic terms and areas of study in the life sciences. Anatomy refers to the internal and external structures of the body and their physical relationships, whereas physiology refers to the study of the functions of those structures.

Concept note-2: -First, I have used “structure/function” to emphasize the importance of understanding the arrangement of the system components (structure or anatomy) that gives rise to a mechanism or response (function or physiology).

Concept note-3: -Whereas anatomy is about structure, physiology is about function. Human physiology is the scientific study of the chemistry and physics of the structures of the body and the ways in which they work together to support the functions of life.

Concept note-4: -anatomy, a field in the biological sciences concerned with the identification and description of the body structures of living things. Gross anatomy involves the study of major body structures by dissection and observation and in its narrowest sense is concerned only with the human body.

Concept note-5: -What is Anatomy and Physiology? Anatomy is the study of the structures associated with the human body. Physiology is the study of the function of each of these structures. The human body is often thought of as a complicated machine.
