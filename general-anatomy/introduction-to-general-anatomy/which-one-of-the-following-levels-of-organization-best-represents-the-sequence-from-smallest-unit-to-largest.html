Which one of the following levels of organization best represents the sequence from smallest unit to largest?

(A) Cell, organism, organ, tissue, organ system

(B) Cell, tissue, organism, organ system, organ

(C) Organ, organism, tissue, organ system, cell

(D) ** Cell, tissue, organ, organ system, organism

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Six general levels of the organization listed from smallest to largest are chemical, cellular, tissue, organ, organ system, and organism levels.

Concept note-2: -Key Points. Cells are the most basic unit of life at the smallest level of organization.

Concept note-3: -A cell is the smallest independently functioning unit of a living organism.

Concept note-4: -Ascending order means to arrange numbers in increasing order, that is, from smallest to largest. To arrange numbers in any order, we first need to compare them. Arranging numbers in ascending order: Count the number of digits in each number.

Concept note-5: -Answer and Explanation: The correct answer is (a): cell, tissue, organ, organ systems, organism. All animals are multicellular and cells are the smallest living units. Cells come together to form tissues.
