INTRODUCTION TO GENERAL ANATOMY

[SOURCES]
Describes the powers of the president

(A) command armed forces

(B) attack others

(C) ** sign the legislation, command armed forces, ask for the written opinion, and grant representatives

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The primary duty of the president is to preserve, protect and defend the constitution and the law of India per Article 60. The president appoints the Chief Justice of India and other judges on the advice of the chief justice. The President may dismiss a judge with a two-thirds vote of the two Houses of the parliament.

Concept note-2: -The Executive Office of the President (EOP) consists of the immediate staff to the President, along with entities such as the Office of Management and Budget and the Office of the United States Trade Representative.

Concept note-3: -There is a Secretary of Defense, who is the head of the Department of Defense, appointed from civilian life by the President, by and with the advice and consent of the Senate.

Concept note-4: -Treaties, controlling the military, appointing Supreme Court justices, and vetoing legislation are examples of the power of executive. Presidents claim powers that are required to carry out the law.
