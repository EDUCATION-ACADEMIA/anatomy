What is federalism and why is it important

(A) ** The federal principle or system of government, it is important because without it our government would be confusing

(B) a group of people who support britian

(C) People who love to eat cake. Without federalism our government would be confusing

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Federalism can be defined as a type of government where both the central government and state government are combined in a united political system. India opted for federalism because it gave a written and rigid constitution to us. it gave powers to different levels of government.

Concept note-2: -It is a system of government that divides the power between a large central government and the local and regional government were established by the constitution. It is composed of 3 branches of legislative, executive and judicial. Austria, India, Iraq, Germany etc have this type of government.

Concept note-3: -Ans: The federal system thus has dual objectives: to safeguard and promote the unity of the country, and accommodate regional diversity. These aspects are crucial for the institutions and practice of federalism.

Concept note-4: -Federalism is a system of government in which powers have been divided between the centre and its constituent parts such as states or provinces. It is an institutional mechanism to accommodate two sets of politics, one at the centre or national level and second at the regional or provincial level.
