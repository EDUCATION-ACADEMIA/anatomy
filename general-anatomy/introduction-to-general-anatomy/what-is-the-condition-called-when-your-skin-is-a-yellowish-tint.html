What is the condition called when your skin is a yellowish tint?

(A) ** Jaundice

(B) Albino

(C) Cyanosis

(D) Erythema

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Jaundice is a yellow color of the skin, mucus membranes, or eyes. The yellow coloring comes from bilirubin, a byproduct of old red blood cells. Jaundice can be a symptom of several health problems.

Concept note-2: -Excess bilirubin (hyperbilirubinemia) is the main cause of jaundice. Bilirubin, which is responsible for the yellow color of jaundice, is a normal part of the pigment released from the breakdown of “used” red blood cells.

Concept note-3: -This is known as hyperbilirubinemia, and it causes the yellow color in the skin and eyes. Jaundice typically occurs due to an underlying disorder that either causes the production of too much bilirubin or prevents the liver from eliminating it.

Concept note-4: -Yellowish skin is usually caused by jaundice. This condition happens when there is a high level of bilirubin in the blood. Jaundice can be a sign of a serious condition, such as liver disease. Bilirubin is a yellowish compound formed when old or damaged red blood cells break down.

Concept note-5: -Jaundice (JAWN-dis) is when the skin turns yellow. It is caused by having too much of a chemical called bilirubin (bil-ih-RU-bin) in the body. Bilirubin comes from the normal breakdown of red blood cells. It is changed in the liver to a form that can leave the body in your stool.
