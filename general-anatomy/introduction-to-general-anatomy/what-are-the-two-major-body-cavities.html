INTRODUCTION TO GENERAL ANATOMY

What are the two major body cavities?

(A) Thoracic and Ventral

(B) Spinal and Cranial

(C) Pelvic and Abdominopelvic

(D) ** Ventral and Dorsal

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The dorsal cavity contains the spinal cord, central nervous system, and spinal column, whereas the ventral cavity consists of the thoracic, abdominal, and pelvic cavities.

Concept note-2: -Body Cavities The cavities, or spaces, of the body contain the internal organs, or viscera. The two main cavities are called the ventral and dorsal cavities. The ventral is the larger cavity and is subdivided into two parts (thoracic and abdominopelvic cavities) by the diaphragm, a dome-shaped respiratory muscle.

Concept note-3: -Toward the back (not rear): for the pig, dorsal is used; for humans, posterior may also be used. Toward the front/belly: for the pig, ventral is used; for humans, anterior may also be used.

Concept note-4: -In the posterior (dorsal) cavity, the cranial cavity houses the brain, and the spinal cavity (or vertebral cavity) encloses the spinal cord.

Concept note-5: -The dorsal body cavity is located along the dorsal (posterior) surface of the human body, where it is subdivided into the cranial cavity housing the brain and the spinal cavity housing the spinal cord. The two cavities are continuous with one another.
