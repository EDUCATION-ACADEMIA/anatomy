What is congresses primary role

(A) to write the constitution

(B) ** hold hearings to inform the legislative process

(C) hold hearings to inform the executive process

(D) hold hearings to inform the judicial process

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -STEPS IN LEGISLATIVE DRAFTING (1) Understanding, (2) Analysis, (3) Design, (4) Composition, and (5) Scrutiny.

Concept note-2: -Role of the legislature in policy formulation It acts as a medium for the public to debate on the issues and their respective policies. The parliament is the apex body when it comes to finalizing a policy by the virtue of its elected members, who draw their powers from the Indian Constitution.

Concept note-3: -Solution. Introduction of a bill by the members of the Parliament for consideration in either of the two houses of the Parliament. Referring the bill to a standing committee by the Speaker of Lok Sabha for examination. Passing of the bill in the respective house of the Parliament.

Concept note-4: -There are three powers into which legislative powers are divided. State list, union list, and concurrent list. While statutory laws are the basic framework of law that is required by the modern legal system. Furthermore, subordinate legislation and supreme legislation are the two types of the legislature.
