What is the small structure seen in Cytoplasm

(A) Lipid

(B) Golgi

(C) Ribosomes

(D) ** Organelles

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Located freely within the cytoplasm are numerous non-membrane bound organelles known as ribosomes. Ribosomes function to produce proteins within the cell.

Concept note-2: -Final answer: The cytoplasm of a cell has many tiny structures in it called organelles.

Concept note-3: -Cytoplasm Structure Cells have three main elements i.e., plasma membrane, and cytoplasm and the nucleus. The plasma membrane or cell membrane is a bi-lipid membranous layer, parting the cell organelles from its outside environment and from the different cells.

Concept note-4: -membrane – thin tissue that surrounds and contains an organelle. membrane-bound – an organelle that is surrounded by a membrane.

Concept note-5: -Ribosomes are the smallest organelle present in the cell irrespective of the type of the cell. These are a complex of ribosomal RNA molecules and proteins that is responsible to carry out protein synthesis. Ribosomes are spheroidal dense particles ranging from 20-30 nm in diameter.
