NEGATIVE feedback mechanisms include:

(A) Body temperature

(B) control of blood sugar by insulin

(C) thermostats

(D) ** All choices are correct

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -(a) A negative feedback loop has four basic parts: A stimulus, sensor, control, and effector. (b) Body temperature is regulated by negative feedback.

Concept note-2: -Negative feedback loops occur in a series of steps. You have a stimulus, in which a change occurs. You have a sensor, or the change is detected. There’s a control, which is just a response to the change.

Concept note-3: -Final Answer: Constriction of skin blood vessels and contraction of skeletal muscles when it is too cold is an example of a negative feedback loop in humans.

Concept note-4: -Stimulus. Produces change in variable. Sensor/receptor. Detects variable change & sends info to control center. Integration/control center. Receives info from sensor & makes decision about solution. Effector. Carries out control center decision. Response. Return to homeostasis.
