INTRODUCTION TO GENERAL ANATOMY

[SOURCES]
The greater a habitat’s biodiversity, the greater will be that habitat’s-

(A) ** sustainability over time with varying conditions.

(B) consumption of energy in the form of sunlight.

(C) temperature ranges across the seasons

(D) distance to the nearest water source.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Why does biodiversity contribute to a habitat’s sustainability? The more plants and animals in a habitat, the greater the biomass, which is how habitats store energy. The greater the number of species, the more competition there is for food between predators, which sustains their populations.

Concept note-2: -Greater biodiversity in ecosystems, species, and individuals leads to greater stability. For example, species with high genetic diversity and many populations that are adapted to a wide variety of conditions are more likely to be able to weather disturbances, disease, and climate change.

Concept note-3: -Biodiversity contributes to the sustainability of a habitat since a healthy ecology features many trophic levels of producers, consumers, and decomposers. More biodiveristy at any such niche supports each other niche: more diverse plant life leads to more diverse herbivores and in turn more diverse carnivores.

Concept note-4: -The ocean ecosystem has a high sustainability because of-the large variety of different organisms.

Concept note-5: -In general, the variety of distinct habitats found in an ecosystem or biome is referred to as habitat diversity. The term biodiversity refers to the full range of living systems, including genetic diversity, habitat diversity and species diversity.
