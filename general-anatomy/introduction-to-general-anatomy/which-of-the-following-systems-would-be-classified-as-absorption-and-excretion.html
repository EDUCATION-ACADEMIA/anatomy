Which of the following systems would be classified as absorption and excretion?

(A) Male Reproductive system

(B) ** Urinary system

(C) Cardiovascular system

(D) Lymphatic system

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Your urinary system, also called the renal system or urinary tract, removes waste from your blood in the form of urine. It also helps regulate your blood volume and pressure, and controls the level of chemicals and salts (electrolytes) in your body’s cells and blood.

Concept note-2: -Waste Ions and Hydrogen Ions Secreted from the Blood Complete the Formation of Urine. The filtrate absorbed in the glomerulus flows through the renal tubule, where nutrients and water are reabsorbed into capillaries. At the same time, waste ions and hydrogen ions pass from the capillaries into the renal tubule.

Concept note-3: -Although the urinary system has a major role in excretion, other organs contribute to the excretory function. The lungs in the respiratory system excrete some waste products, such as carbon dioxide and water. The skin is another excretory organ that rids the body of wastes through the sweat glands.

Concept note-4: -The urinary system’s function is to filter blood and create urine as a waste by-product. The organs of the urinary system include the kidneys, renal pelvis, ureters, bladder and urethra. The body takes nutrients from food and converts them to energy.
