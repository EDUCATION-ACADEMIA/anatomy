When the dermis pulls away from the epidermis due to pressure, a painful ____ forms.

(A) corn

(B) bunion

(C) ** blister

(D) pimple

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -an open wound or blister – a category 2 pressure ulcer. a deep wound that reaches the deeper layers of the skin – a category 3 pressure ulcer. a very deep wound that may reach the muscle and bone – a category 4 pressure ulcer.

Concept note-2: -They are caused when an area of skin is placed under pressure. They are sometimes known as “bedsores” or “pressure sores". Pressure ulcers can range in severity from patches of discoloured skin to open wounds that expose the underlying bone or muscle.

Concept note-3: -Stage I: A reddened, painful area on the skin that does not turn white (blanch) when pressed. This is a sign that a pressure ulcer may be forming. The skin may be warm or cool, firm or soft. Stage II: The skin blisters or forms an open sore.

Concept note-4: -Injuries to the superficial layers of the skin caused by friction are not pressure ulcers and should not be classified or treated as such.
