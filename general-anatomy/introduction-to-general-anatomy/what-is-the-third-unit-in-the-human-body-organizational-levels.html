What is the third unit in the human body organizational levels?

(A) ** Organs

(B) Body Systems

(C) Cells

(D) Tissues

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Therefore, molecules combine to form cells, cells combine to form tissues, tissues combine to form organs, organs combine to form organ systems, and organ systems combine to form organisms.

Concept note-2: -Tissues are further organized in organs, the third level of organization in living things. Organs are groups of different tissues that work together. Your heart, for example, is an organ made up of muscle tissue, blood tissue, and nerve tissue.

Concept note-3: -The organ level of organization is when two or more tissues work together for a specific function. For example, the bladder consists of an inner lining of epithelial tissue, bound by various connective tissues to (smooth) muscle.

Concept note-4: -Answer and Explanation: The five levels of human body organization are cells, tissues, organs, organ systems and organisms.
