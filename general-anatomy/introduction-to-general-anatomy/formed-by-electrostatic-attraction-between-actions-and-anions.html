Formed by electrostatic attraction between actions and anions

(A) Hydrogen bonds

(B) ** Ionic bonds

(C) Nonpolar covalent

(D) Polar covalent bonds

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Chemical compounds in which anions and cations are held together by electrostatic force of attraction are known as ionic compounds. Cations and anions are charged species which are formed by losing and gaining electron(s) respectively to attain the nearest noble gas configuration or stability.

Concept note-2: -The ionic bond is the electrostatic force of attraction between a positively charged metal ion and a negatively charged non-metal ion. Metals form positive ions because they lose electrons to become stable.

Concept note-3: -The atom losing one or more electrons becomes a cation-a positively charged ion. The atom gaining one or more electron becomes an anion-a negatively charged ion. When the transfer of electrons occurs, an electrostatic attraction between the two ions of opposite charge takes place and an ionic bond is formed.
