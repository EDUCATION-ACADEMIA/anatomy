Which does Boyles law state?

(A) Volume is proportional to pressure

(B) ** Volume is inversely proportional to pressure

(C) Volume is proportional to the square of the pressure

(D) Volume is inversely proportional to the square of the pressure

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Boyle’s law is a gas law which states that the pressure exerted by a gas (of a given mass, kept at a constant temperature) is inversely proportional to the volume occupied by it.

Concept note-2: -Boyles Law states that pressure of a gas is inversely proportional to its volume.

Concept note-3: -Boyle’s law states that the volume of a given mass of gas varies inversely with the pressure when the temperature is kept constant. An inverse relationship is described in this way. As one variable increases in value, the other variable decreases.

Concept note-4: -For a fixed mass of an ideal gas kept at a fixed temperature, pressure and volume are inversely proportional. Or Boyle’s law is a gas law, stating that the pressure and volume of a gas have an inverse relationship. If volume increases, then pressure decreases and vice versa, when the temperature is held constant.

Concept note-5: -The law itself can be stated as follows: For a fixed amount of an ideal gas kept at a fixed temperature, pressure and volume are inversely proportional.
