The Trachea splits into 2 branches and each of these goes into 1 of the lungs.

(A) Bronchioles

(B) Alveoli

(C) ** Bronchi

(D) Esophagus

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Your trachea divides into your left and right bronchi. The bronchi carry air into your lungs. At the end of the bronchi, the bronchioles carry air to small sacs in your lungs called alveoli.

Concept note-2: -In the mediastinum, at the level of the fifth thoracic vertebra, the trachea divides into the right and left primary bronchi. The bronchi branch into smaller and smaller passageways until they terminate in tiny air sacs called alveoli.

Concept note-3: -The human trachea (windpipe) divides into two main bronchi (also called mainstem bronchi), at the anatomical point known as the carina. The right main bronchus is wider and shorter than the left main bronchus. The right main bronchus subdivides into three lobar bronchi and the left main bronchus divides into two.

Concept note-4: -The trachea begins just under the larynx (voice box) and runs down behind the breastbone (sternum). The trachea then divides into two smaller tubes called bronchi: one bronchus for each lung.

Concept note-5: -The trachea extends from the neck and divides into two main bronchi. Structurally similar to the trachea, the two primary bronchi are located inside the lungs. The right bronchus is slightly larger than the left one.
