GENERAL ANATOMY

RESPIRATORY SYSTEM

[SOURCES]
We breathe in oxygen and breathe out ____

(A) Mouth

(B) ** Carbon dioxide

(C) Nose

(D) Water

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The amount of inhaled air contains 21% of oxygen and 0.04% of carbon dioxide, while the air we breathe out contains 16.4% of oxygen and 4.4% of carbon dioxide. This is because our cells use oxygen from the inhaled air to release energy and give out carbon dioxide as a byproduct.

Concept note-2: -When you inhale (breathe in), air enters your lungs, and oxygen from that air moves to your blood. At the same time, carbon dioxide, a waste gas, moves from your blood to the lungs and is exhaled (breathed out). This process, called gas exchange, is essential to life.

Concept note-3: -The role of the respiratory system is to breathe in oxygen and breathe out carbon dioxide. This is known as respiration. The cells of the body use oxygen to perform functions that keep us alive. The waste product created by the cells once they have performed these functions is carbon dioxide.

Concept note-4: -The statement is wrong. The air we inhale has more of oxygen and less of carbon dioxide. The air we exhale has higher concentration of carbon dioxide and lesser concentration of oxygen. Was this answer helpful?
