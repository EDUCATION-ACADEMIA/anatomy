RESPIRATORY SYSTEM

[SOURCES]
Where is air warmed and moistened in the respiratory system?

(A) Trachea

(B) Pharynx

(C) ** Nasal Cavity

(D) Epiglottis

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In the nostrils, air gets warmed and moistened. Tiny hairs in the nose called cilia (SIL-ee-uh) filter out dust and other particles. The nasal cavity and mouth join at the area in the back of the throat called the pharynx (FAR-inks).

Concept note-2: -The nasal passages filter and warm the air, and make it moist before it goes into the lungs. They also contain special cells that are involved in the sense of smell.

Concept note-3: -As the air passes through the nasal cav ity, mucus and hairs trap any particles in the air. The air is also warmed and moistened so it won’t harm delicate tissues of the lungs. Next, the air passes through the pharynx, a long tube that is shared with the digestive system.

Concept note-4: -Turbinates (conchae): There are three pairs of turbinates located along the sides of both nasal cavities. These folds inside your nose help warm and moisten air after you breathe it in and help with nasal drainage.

Concept note-5: -The nasal cavity is the inside of your nose. It is lined with a mucous membrane that helps keep your nose moist by making mucus so you won’t get nosebleeds from a dry nose. There are also little hairs that help filter the air you breathe in, blocking dirt and dust from getting into your lungs.
