What respiratory disorders are highly contagious?

(A) ** influenza and URI

(B) sinusitis and epistaxis

(C) emphysema and asthma

(D) laryngitis and pleurisy

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -RSV is the most common cause of respiratory tract illness in children under 2 years of age; it is the major cause of bronchiolitis, pneumonia, croup, bronchitis, and otitis media. The influenza viruses are highly contagious and can cause large epidemics.

Concept note-2: -An upper respiratory infection (URI) affects the nasal passages and throat. The treatment is usually simple unless a person also has a chronic respiratory condition, such as asthma. A URI occurs when a virus or bacteria enter the body, usually through the mouth or nose.

Concept note-3: -Upper respiratory infections include: Common cold. Epiglottitis. Laryngitis.

Concept note-4: -Coronavirus Disease 2019 (COVID-19) Diphtheria. Enterovirus D68. Influenza (flu) Haemophilus influenzae type b. Legionellosis (Legionella): Legionnaires’ Disease and Pontiac Fever. Measles (rubeola) Middle East Respiratory Syndrome Coronavirus (MERS-CoV) More items •07-Dec-2022
