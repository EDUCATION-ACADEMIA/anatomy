Two organs that filter blood cells are called

(A) ** Kidneys

(B) Lungs

(C) Heart

(D) Liver

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The kidneys are two bean-shaped organs that filter your blood. Your kidneys are part of your urinary system. Your kidneys filter about 200 quarts of fluid every day-enough to fill a large bathtub. During this process, your kidneys remove waste, which leaves your body as urine (pee).

Concept note-2: -You have two kidneys that filter your blood, removing wastes and extra water to make urine.

Concept note-3: -Their main job is to cleanse the blood of toxins and transform the waste into urine. Each kidney weighs about 160 grams and gets rid of between one and one-and-a-half litres of urine per day. The two kidneys together filter 200 litres of fluid every 24 hours. to the blood.

Concept note-4: -Your liver has many important jobs. One job is to act as a “filter” for your body. The liver filters or detoxifies the blood. Almost all the blood in your body passes through the liver.

Concept note-5: -The kidneys remove urea from the blood through tiny filtering units called nephrons. Each nephron consists of a ball formed of small blood capillaries, called a glomerulus, and a small tube called a renal tubule.
