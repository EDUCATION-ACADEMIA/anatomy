Which of the following statements describes the function of the diaphragm?

(A) ** It contracts and relaxes during respiration to change the volume of the chest cavity

(B) It serves as the passage of air into the lungs

(C) It carries blood throughout the body

(D) It is the site of gas exchange

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -d. It is the site of gas exchange Diaphragm is the muscle at the base of the lungs which aids during exhalation and inhalation. When the diaphragm muscle contract, it pulls downwards creating more space on the surrounding of the lungs together with the rib cage expanding.

Concept note-2: -The diaphragm is a thin skeletal muscle that sits at the base of the chest and separates the abdomen from the chest. It contracts and flattens when you inhale. This creates a vacuum effect that pulls air into the lungs. When you exhale, the diaphragm relaxes and the air is pushed out of lungs.

Concept note-3: -Correct option: B. Explanation: When the chest expands air enters the lungs. This is because during inspiration the diaphragm contracts and moves downwards.

Concept note-4: -Diaphragm helps in breathing by moving up and down. In inhalation, the diaphragm becomes flat and moves downwards. In exhalation, diaphragm relaxes and moves upwards.
