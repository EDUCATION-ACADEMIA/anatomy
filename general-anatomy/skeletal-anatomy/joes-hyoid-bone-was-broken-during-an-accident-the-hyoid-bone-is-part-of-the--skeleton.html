(A) ** Axial

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The five parts of your axial skeleton include the bones in your skull, ossicles (small bones) of your middle ear, hyoid bone of your neck, vertebra (bones of your spine) and thoracic cage (ribcage).

Concept note-2: -Although it is not found in the skull, the hyoid bone is considered a component of the axial skeleton. The hyoid bone lies below the mandible in the front of the neck. It acts as a movable base for the tongue and is connected to muscles of the jaw, larynx, and tongue.

Concept note-3: -In spite of the fact that is not attached to the skull, it is considered part of the axial skeleton, specifically part of the skull. It has a shape similar to the mandible, suggesting a common origin. Functionally it is important because it is the origin of muscles that move the larynx during the act of swallowing.

Concept note-4: -The axial skeleton includes the bones that form the skull, laryngeal skeleton, vertebral column, and thoracic cage.
