Bones in your fingers are called the ____

(A) clavicle

(B) ** phalanges

(C) humerus

(D) radius

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Phalanges. The 14 bones that are found in the fingers of each hand and also in the toes of each foot. Each finger has 3 phalanges (the distal, middle, and proximal); the thumb only has 2.

Concept note-2: -The phalanges of the hand are the group of small bones that comprise the bony core of the digits (fingers) of the hand. Even though the phalanges are small in size, they are classified as long bones because of their structural characteristics; each phalanx consists of a shaft, distal head and a proximal base.

Concept note-3: -The phalanges of the hand are commonly known as the finger bones.

Concept note-4: -Eachfinger, starting with the one closest to the thumb, has a colloquial name to distinguish it from the others: index finger, pointer finger, or forefinger. middle finger or long finger, ring finger. little finger, pinky finger, or small finger.

Concept note-5: -What are Phalanges? Our fingers and toes are home to some small yet important bones. These groups of bones, called the phalanges, give us the structure needed to complete tasks like typing, tying your shoes, or even standing on your tiptoes.
