What is the purpose of the rib cage?

(A) Helps us to think

(B) make blood for the body

(C) Protects our brain

(D) ** Protects our vital organs like heart and lungs

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The rib cage help protects the organs in the chest, such as the heart and lungs, from damage.

Concept note-2: -Hint: The rib cage protects the internal organs forming a protective cage around the thorax of the chest and supports the chest wall preventing it from collapsing. The starting 7 pairs of ribs are known as true ribs.-The apex of lungs lies above the extent of the primary rib.

Concept note-3: -Ribs join the chest bone and the backbone together to form a box-like structure called rib cage. Vital organs such as heart and lungs are located and protected inside the rib cage.

Concept note-4: -The rib cage is part of the Axial skeleton. It is made up of rib bones that attach to the sternum in front and the Vertebral column in the back. The rib cageâ€™s most important function is to protect vital organs such as the Heart and Lungs.

Concept note-5: -The ribs are the bony framework of the thoracic cavity. The ribs form the main structure of the thoracic cage protecting the thoracic organs, however their main function is to aid respiration. There are twelve pairs of ribs.
