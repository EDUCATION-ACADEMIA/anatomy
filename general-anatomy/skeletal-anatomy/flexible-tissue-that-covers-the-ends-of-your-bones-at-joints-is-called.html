Flexible tissue that covers the ends of your bones at joints is called

(A) Synovial Fluid

(B) ** Cartilage

(C) Smooth Muscle

(D) Cardiac Muscle

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Hyaline cartilage at the ends of your bones is sometimes referred to as articular cartilage . Hyaline cartilage is slippery and smooth which helps your bones move smoothly past each other in your joints. Itâ€™s flexible but strong enough to help your joints hold their shape.

Concept note-2: -Hyaline Cartilage Articular cartilage covers the ends of all bones found in joints (knee, ankle, hip, shoulder, elbow, wrist and finger) and allows them to slide freely in the joint.

Concept note-3: -Cartilage is an important structural component of the body. It is a firm tissue but is softer and much more flexible than bone. Cartilage is a connective tissue found in many areas of the body including: Joints between bones e.g. the elbows, knees and ankles.

Concept note-4: -Hyaline Cartilage: Hyaline cartilage is the most abundant type of cartilage. Hyaline cartilage is found lining bones in joints (articular cartilage). It is also present inside bones, serving as a center of ossification or bone growth. In addition, hyaline cartilage forms the embryonic skeleton.

Concept note-5: -Cartilage is a flexible connective tissue that differs from bone in several ways; it is avascular and its microarchitecture is less organized than bone. The cells (chondrocytes) are scattered and lie firmly fixed in matrix supported by collagen and elastic fibres.
