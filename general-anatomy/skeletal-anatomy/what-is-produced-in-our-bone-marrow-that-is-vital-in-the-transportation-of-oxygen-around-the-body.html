What is produced in our Bone Marrow that is vital in the transportation of oxygen around the body?

(A) White Blood Cells

(B) Lympocytes

(C) ** Red Blood Cells

(D) Hormones

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Red blood cells grow in your bone marrow. Bone marrow creates almost all of the cells in your body. Red blood cells contain a protein called hemoglobin, which is responsible for carrying oxygen.

Concept note-2: -Red blood cells are an important element of blood. Their job is to transport oxygen to the bodyâ€™s tissues in exchange for carbon dioxide, which they carry to the lungs to be expelled. Red blood cells are formed in the red bone marrow of bones.

Concept note-3: -Red bone marrow contains blood stem cells that can become red blood cells, white blood cells, or platelets. Yellow bone marrow is made mostly of fat and contains stem cells that can become cartilage, fat, or bone cells. Anatomy of the bone.

Concept note-4: -Most blood cells are made in your bone marrow. This process is called haemopoiesis.

Concept note-5: -Haemoglobin molecules inside red blood cells pick up and carry the oxygen. These oxygen-rich cells travel in the blood vessels from the lungs to the left side of the heart. The blood is then pumped around the body. Red blood cells are adapted for the transport of oxygen.
