(A) Fibrous layer

(B) ** Osteogenic layer

(C) Endosteum

(D) Intercellular substance

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -They are part of the outer fibrous layer of periosteum, entering into the outer circumferential and interstitial lamellae of bone tissue. Sharpey’s fibres are also used to attach muscle to the periosteum of bone by merging with the fibrous periosteum and underlying bone as well.

Concept note-2: -1) Cementum contains two types of fibers, i.e. extrinsic (Sharpey’s) fibers which are embedded ends of the principal fibers and intrinsic fibers which are fibers of cementum proper.

Concept note-3: -Alveolar bone can be divided into two main parts; a thin layer of compact (radiodense) bone that lines the alveolus proper, and in which Sharpey’s fibers insert, that is termed the lamina dura (lamina dura denta).
