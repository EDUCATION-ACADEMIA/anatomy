A muscle and ligament projection that is very small and rounded.

(A) tuberosity

(B) ** tubercle

(C) fossa

(D) head

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A tuberosity is a large projection on the surface of the bone that is rough but can be rounded and serves as an attachment site for muscles and ligaments.

Concept note-2: -The correct answer is condyle. A condyle is a small, rounded projection or prominence at the end of the bone and often appears in joints where they articulate with other bones such as at the knee and at the temporomandibular joint.

Concept note-3: -Tubercle-A small, rounded prominence where connective tissues attach. Examples include the greater and lesser tubercle of the humerus.

Concept note-4: -Condyles-rounded ends or projections of bone. Epicondyle-a projection above a condyle (epi = on or above) Facets-smooth articular surfaces.

Concept note-5: -Ramus. rounded, articular projection, takes part in joint formation. Condyle. narrow opening, a passageway for nerves or blood vessels.
