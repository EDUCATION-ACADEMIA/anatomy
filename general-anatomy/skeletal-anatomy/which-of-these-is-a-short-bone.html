Which of these is a short bone?

(A) Femur

(B) ** Patella

(C) Scapula

(D) Ulna

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The outside surface is comprised of a thin layer of compact bone. Short bones are located in the hands and feet. The patella (kneecap) is also considered a short bone.

Concept note-2: -The patella is the largest sesamoid bone in the human body and is located anterior to knee joint within the tendon of the quadriceps femoris muscle, providing an attachment point for both the quadriceps tendon and the patellar ligament.

Concept note-3: -Short bones are located in the hands and feet. The patella (kneecap) is also considered a short bone.

Concept note-4: -Structure and Function In the lower extremities, the patella is found in the knee and is the largest sesamoid bone.
