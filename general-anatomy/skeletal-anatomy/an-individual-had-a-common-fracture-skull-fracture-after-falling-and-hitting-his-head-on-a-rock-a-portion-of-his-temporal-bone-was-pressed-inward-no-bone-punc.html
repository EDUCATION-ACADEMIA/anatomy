An individual had a common fracture skull fracture after falling and hitting his head on a rock. A portion of his temporal bone was pressed inward. No bone punctured the skin.

(A) Greenstick

(B) Comminuted

(C) ** Depression

(D) Spiral

(E) Compacted

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A skull fracture is a head injury where there is a break in the skull bone. While mild breaks can cause few problems and heal over time, severe breaks can lead to complications including bleeding, brain damage, leaking of cerebrospinal fluid, infection and seizures.

Concept note-2: -A temporal bone fracture may cause facial paralysis, hearing loss, bruising behind the ear, and bleeding from the ear. Doctors use computed tomography (CT) to diagnose temporal bone fractures. Treatment, sometimes including surgery, is needed if the fracture causes problems.

Concept note-3: -A depressed skull fracture is a type of fracture usually resulting from blunt force trauma, such as getting struck with a hammer, rock or getting kicked in the head.

Concept note-4: -Linear skull fracture This is the most common type of skull fracture. In a linear fracture, there is a break in the bone, but the bone does not move. Patients with these fractures may be observed in the hospital for a brief amount of time and can usually resume normal activities in a few days.
