SKELETAL ANATOMY

extending the foot at the ankle, bending the foot downward

(A) ** plantar flexion

(B) dorsiflexion

(C) hyperextension

(D) elevation

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Plantar flexion is the movement that allows you to press the gas pedal of your car. It also allows ballet dancers to stand on their toes. The term plantar flexion refers to the movement of the foot in a downward motion away from the body.

Concept note-2: -In normal function and anatomical position, the ankle joint has extension (dorsiflexion) and flexion (plantar flexion).

Concept note-3: -Plantar flexion describes the extension of the ankle so that the foot points down and away from the leg. When in a standing position, this would mean pointing the foot towards the floor. Plantar flexion has a normal range of motion from about 20 to 50 degrees from the resting position.

Concept note-4: -plantarflexion: Movement of the foot downwards away from the lower leg.

Concept note-5: -Answer and Explanation: Ankle extension is called plantar flexion because you actually flex the muscle called the plantaris muscle.
