A patient who can produce antibodies against some bacterial pathogens, but not against viral infections, probably has a disorder in his

(A) B cells

(B) plasma cells.

(C) ** t cells

(D) macrophages

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -IgG antibodies help protect your body from viral and bacterial infections. Found in your blood and lymph system, IgM antibodies act as the first line of defense against infections. They also play a large role in immune regulation.

Concept note-2: -IgG antibodies inactivate viruses and neutralize bacterial toxins. cells. in the lysis of a bacterial (antigenic) cell.

Concept note-3: -Two types of immunity exist-active and passive: Active immunity occurs when our own immune system is responsible for protecting us from a pathogen. Passive immunity occurs when we are protected from a pathogen by immunity gained from someone else.

Concept note-4: -The antibodies destroy the antigen (pathogen) which is then engulfed and digested by macrophages. White blood cells can also produce chemicals called antitoxins which destroy the toxins (poisons) some bacteria produce when they have invaded the body.
