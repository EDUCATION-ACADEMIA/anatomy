B or T cells that show the potential to mount a strong immune response against self antigens undergo apoptosis. This is called ____

(A) ** clonal deletion

(B) clonal selection

(C) secondary immune response

(D) immunological memory

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Adaptive immune responses are carried out by white blood cells called lymphocytes. There are two broad classes of such responses-antibody responses and cell-mediated immune responses, and they are carried out by different classes of lymphocytes, called B cells and T cells, respectively.

Concept note-2: -In immunology, clonal deletion is the removal through apoptosis of B cells and T cells that have expressed receptors for self before developing into fully immunocompetent lymphocytes. This prevents recognition and destruction of self host cells, making it a type of negative selection or central tolerance.

Concept note-3: -Helper T cells are arguably the most important cells in adaptive immunity, as they are required for almost all adaptive immune responses. They not only help activate B cells to secrete antibodies and macrophages to destroy ingested microbes, but they also help activate cytotoxic T cells to kill infected target cells.

Concept note-4: -Tolerance to self is a normal state that is maintained chiefly by clonal deletion of developing T and B cells and clonal deletion or inactivation of mature peripheral T and B cells.
