Concept note-1: -Hand washing is a key strategy for preventing the spread of infectious diseases. It is particularly important before, during, and after preparing food, as well as after using the toilet. It ensures that harmful pathogens are not spread from sources that are likely to harbor these pathogens to other areas and food.

Concept note-2: -These include: controlled breeding, controlling entry to farm lots, and quarantining sick animals and through developing and improving antibiotics, vaccines and diagnostic tools, evaluation of ethno-therapeutic options, and vector control techniques.

Concept note-3: -Regular annual vaccination of animals in endemic areas will prevent the disease from occurring. Vaccination may be carried out at least a month prior to expected disease occurrence in endemic areas.

Concept note-4: -The best way to avoid diseases acquired from contact with animals is to thoroughly wash hands with soap and water after contact with animals or their environments.
