GENERAL ANATOMY

IMMUNE SYSTEM

[SOURCES]
Vaccines take advantage of this type of immunity

(A) Humoral

(B) Innate

(C) Passive

(D) ** Acquired

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Vaccine-induced immunity is acquired through the introduction of a killed or weakened form of the disease organism through vaccination.

Concept note-2: -If you’re wondering why vaccine-induced immunity is considered a form of active immunity, while immune globulin shots are passive, it is because immune globulin is a substance made up of antibodies that are naturally made by the body to provide protection from certain diseases.

Concept note-3: -The goal of vaccination is to induce long-term protective immunity, which is a hallmark of adaptive immunity. In contrast, innate immunity does not have immunological memory.

Concept note-4: -When the active substances in the vaccine meet the body’s immune system, immune cells and antibodies are produced that will recognise the “real” microbe. When the vaccinated person encounters this microbe, the immune system will provide a faster and better immune response that can prevent disease.

Concept note-5: -a vaccine. exposure to an infection or disease. another person’s antibodies (infection-fighting immune cells) 09-Dec-2019
