____ are white blood cells that fight infection by “eating” or engulfing pathogens.

(A) T-cells

(B) B-cells

(C) ** Phagocytes

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A type of white blood cell, granulocyte, and phagocyte that aids in fighting infection. Neutrophils kill pathogens by ingesting them. Phagocytes eat up pathogens by attaching to and wrapping around the pathogen to engulf it. Once the pathogen is trapped inside the phagocyte, it is in a compartment called a phagosome.

Concept note-2: -Macrophages and neutrophils (phagocytes) are the front-line defenders in your body’s immune system. They seek out, ingest, and destroy pathogens and other debris through a process called phagocytosis.

Concept note-3: -Monocytes, macrophages, and neutrophils are phagocytes. A phagocyte is a type of white blood cell.

Concept note-4: -Phagocytes (neutrophils and monocytes) are immune cells that play a critical role in both the early and late stages of immune responses. Their main role is to circulate and migrate through tissues to ingest and destroy both microbes and cellular debris.
