IMMUNE SYSTEM

[SOURCES]
T cells are made in the ____

(A) ** bone marrow

(B) spleen

(C) thymus

(D) tonsiles

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -T lymphocytes develop from a common lymphoid progenitor in the bone marrow that also gives rise to B lymphocytes, but those progeny destined to give rise to T cells leave the bone marrow and migrate to the thymus (see Fig. 7.2). This is the reason they are called thymus-dependent (T) lymphocytes or T cells.

Concept note-2: -T lymphocytes originate from bone marrow progenitors that migrate to the thymus for maturation, selection, and subsequent export to the periphery.

Concept note-3: -Organs and Tissues The bone marrow is extremely important to the immune system because all the bodyâ€™s blood cells (including T and B lymphocytes) originate in the bone marrow. B lymphocytes remain in the marrow to mature, while T lymphocytes travel to the thymus.

Concept note-4: -Red bone marrow contains blood stem cells that can become red blood cells, white blood cells, or platelets. Yellow bone marrow is made mostly of fat and contains stem cells that can become cartilage, fat, or bone cells.
