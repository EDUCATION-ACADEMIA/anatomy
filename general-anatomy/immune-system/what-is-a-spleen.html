What is a spleen?

(A) Small organs in the back of the throat.

(B) The spongy tissue inside some of the bones in the body.

(C) ** Controls the amount of red blood cells and blood storage in the body

(D) Help the immune system fight off illness.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The spleen acts as a filter. It weeds out old and damaged cells and helps control the amount of blood and blood cells that circulate in the body. The spleen also helps get rid of germs. It contains white blood cells called lymphocytes and macrophages.

Concept note-2: -The spleen controls the level of white blood cells, red blood cells and platelets (small cells that form blood clots). It screens the blood and removes any old or damaged red blood cells.

Concept note-3: -The spleen helps keep harmful microorganisms out of the bloodstream. It holds key components of the bodyâ€™s immune system. The spleen also removes unhealthy, old, and misshapen red blood cells from circulation. Red blood cells carry oxygen throughout the body and remove carbon dioxide (a waste product).

Concept note-4: -About 25% to 30% of red blood cells (RBCs) are stored in the spleen, along with about 25% of platelets normally sequestered in the spleen.

Concept note-5: -The spleen filters your blood and also stores your red blood cells, platelets, and white blood cells. But if your spleen is removed, other parts of the lymphatic system can take over these functions.
