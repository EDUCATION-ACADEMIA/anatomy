Which of the following blood groups is known as the universal acceptor?

(A) A

(B) B

(C) ** AB

(D) O

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -AB+ is the universal acceptor as the blood group contains A, B and Rh antigens; contains no antibodies in its serum.

Concept note-2: -Individuals with the AB blood group are known as universal recipients because they can receive blood from all blood kinds without experiencing any hemolytic reactions.

Concept note-3: -Because it has no antibodies to A, B, and Rh, the AB-is a universal acceptor and may absorb red blood cells from any kind of donor.

Concept note-4: -AB+ donors are universal plasma and platelet donors, meaning anyone can receive their plasma and platelets.

Concept note-5: -People with type AB blood are universal plasma donors. Their plasma does not contain A or B antibodies and can be transfused safely to all blood types.
