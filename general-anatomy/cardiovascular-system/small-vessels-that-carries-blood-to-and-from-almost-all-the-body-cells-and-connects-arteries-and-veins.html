Small vessels that carries blood to and from almost all the body cells and connects arteries and veins

(A) Arterioles

(B) Arteries

(C) Veins

(D) Venules

(E) ** Capillaries

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Capillaries, the smallest blood vessels, connect arteries and veins.

Concept note-2: -Capillaries are small, thin blood vessels that connect the arteries and the veins. Their thin walls allow oxygen, nutrients, carbon dioxide and waste products to pass to and from the tissue cells.

Concept note-3: -Blood travels from the heart in arteries, which branch into smaller and smaller vessels, eventually becoming arterioles. Arterioles connect with even smaller blood vessels called capillaries.

Concept note-4: -Arterioles carry blood and oxygen into the smallest blood vessels, the capillaries. Capillaries are so small they can only be seen under a microscope. The walls of the capillaries are permeable to oxygen and carbon dioxide. Oxygen moves from the capillary toward the cells of the tissues and organs.

Concept note-5: -Capillaries, the smallest and most numerous of the blood vessels, form the connection between the vessels that carry blood away from the heart (arteries) and the vessels that return blood to the heart (veins). The primary function of capillaries is the exchange of materials between the blood and tissue cells.
