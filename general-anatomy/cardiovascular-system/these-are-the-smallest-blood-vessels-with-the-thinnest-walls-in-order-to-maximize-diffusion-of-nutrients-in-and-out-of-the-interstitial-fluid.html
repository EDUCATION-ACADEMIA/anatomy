These are the smallest blood vessels with the thinnest walls in order to maximize diffusion of nutrients in and out of the interstitial fluid

(A) arteries

(B) veins

(C) ** capillaries

(D) aorta

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Capillaries are thin-walled vessels composed of a single endothelial layer. Because of the thin walls of the capillary, the exchange of nutrients and metabolites occurs primarily via diffusion.

Concept note-2: -Capillaries: These tiny blood vessels have thin walls. Oxygen and nutrients from the blood can move through the walls and get into organs and tissues. The capillaries also take waste products away from your tissues.

Concept note-3: -Arterioles carry blood and oxygen into the smallest blood vessels, the capillaries. Capillaries are so small they can only be seen under a microscope. The walls of the capillaries are permeable to oxygen and carbon dioxide. Oxygen moves from the capillary toward the cells of the tissues and organs.

Concept note-4: -Capillaries, the Smallest Blood Vessels, Are the Sites for the Exchange of Water and Solutes Between the Bloodstream and the Interstitial Fluid. Because of their small size, the capillaries are sometimes called the microcirculation.

Concept note-5: -Capillaries. Capillaries are tiny, extremely thin-walled vessels that act as a bridge between arteries (which carry blood away from the heart) and veins (which carry blood back to the heart).
