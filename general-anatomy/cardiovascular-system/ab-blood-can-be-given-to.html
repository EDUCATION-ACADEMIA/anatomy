CARDIOVASCULAR SYSTEM

[SOURCES]
AB blood can be given to

(A) A

(B) B

(C) both A & B

(D) ** only AB

(E) every type of blood

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Group AB can donate to other AB’s but can receive from all others. Group B can donate red blood cells to B’s and AB’s.

Concept note-2: -Type AB-negative blood is considered a universal plasma donor, meaning anyone can receive type AB-negative plasma.

Concept note-3: -So what makes AB+ different from the other blood types? AB+ blood has both A and B antigens at the surface of the red blood cells, while other blood groups (A and B) only have one, or lack them altogether (group O). Because of this unique combination, AB+ donors’ blood can only be given to others with AB+ blood.

Concept note-4: -Who can receive AB positive blood? AB positive red blood cells can only be used to treat people with AB positive blood.

Concept note-5: -Blood B+ can donate to B+ and AB+ Blood B-can donate to B+, B-, AB+ and AB-Blood AB+ can donate to AB+
