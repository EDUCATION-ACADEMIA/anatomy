How many valves does your heart have?

(A) 1

(B) 2

(C) 3

(D) ** 4

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -There are four valves in the heart: aortic valve, mitral valve, tricuspid valve, and pulmonary valve.

Concept note-2: -The heart has four heart valves â€“ the aortic, mitral, pulmonary and tricuspid valves. All four valves open and close to help move blood from one area to another.

Concept note-3: -tricuspid valve: located between the right atrium and the right ventricle. pulmonary valve: located between the right ventricle and the pulmonary artery. mitral valve: located between the left atrium and the left ventricle. aortic valve: located between the left ventricle and the aorta.

Concept note-4: -The four chambers of the heart There are four chambers: the left atrium and right atrium (upper chambers), and the left ventricle and right ventricle (lower chambers). The right side of your heart collects blood on its return from the rest of your body.

Concept note-5: -A normal heart and heart valve problems The four heart valves, which keep blood flowing in the right direction, are the mitral, tricuspid, pulmonary and aortic valves. Each valve has flaps (leaflets) that open and close once per heartbeat.
