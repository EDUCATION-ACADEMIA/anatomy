The fluid in which other parts of the blood are suspended

(A) Platelets

(B) Blood

(C) ** Plasma

(D) Hemoglobin

(E) White Blood Cells

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Blood is fluid tissue. It consists of the liquid part called Blood plasma. Blood cells are suspended in the plasma.

Concept note-2: -Plasma. This is the liquid part of blood. The following blood cells are suspended in plasma: Red blood cells (erythrocytes).

Concept note-3: -Plasma, also known as blood plasma, appears light-yellowish or straw-colored. It serves as the liquid base for whole blood. Whole blood minus erythrocytes (RBCs), leukocytes (WBCs), and thrombocytes (platelets) make up the plasma.

Concept note-4: -Blood cells are suspended in the plasma.

Concept note-5: -Plasma is the liquid portion of blood. Plasma is a pale yellowish, watery substance that surrounds blood cells and carries them around the body. Plasma is a combination or mixture of nutrients, gases, proteins, hormones, salts, and wastes.
