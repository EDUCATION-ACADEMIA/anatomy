Which of the following includes two types of cells found in blood?

(A) Platelets and capillaries

(B) Red blood cells and antigens

(C) Red blood cells and capillaries

(D) ** White blood cells and platelets

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -There are two main classes of cells, the B cells that mature in the bone marrow, and the T cells that mature in the thymus gland.

Concept note-2: -Blood contains many types of cells: white blood cells (monocytes, lymphocytes, neutrophils, eosinophils, basophils, and macrophages), red blood cells (erythrocytes), and platelets.

Concept note-3: -Types of white blood cells are granulocytes (neutrophils, eosinophils, and basophils), monocytes, and lymphocytes (T cells and B cells). Checking the number of white blood cells in the blood is usually part of a complete blood cell (CBC) test.

Concept note-4: -Your blood is made up of red blood cells, white blood cells, platelets, and plasma. Your white blood cells account for only about 1% of your blood, but their impact is big. White blood cells are also called leukocytes. They protect you against illness and disease.

Concept note-5: -White blood cell formation occurs in the soft tissue inside of your bones (bone marrow). Two types of white blood cells (lymphocytes) grow in the thymus gland (T cells) and lymph nodes and spleen (B cells).
