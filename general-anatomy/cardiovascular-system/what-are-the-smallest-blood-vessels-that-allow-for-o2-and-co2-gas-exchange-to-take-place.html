What are the smallest blood vessels that allow for O2 and CO2 gas exchange to take place

(A) arteries

(B) veins

(C) ** capillaries

(D) venules

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Capillaries: These tiny blood vessels have thin walls. Oxygen and nutrients from the blood can move through the walls and get into organs and tissues. The capillaries also take waste products away from your tissues. Capillaries are where oxygen and nutrients are exchanged for carbon dioxide and waste.

Concept note-2: -Capillaries-these are the sites of gas exchange between the tissues.

Concept note-3: -At each body part, a network of tiny blood vessels called capillaries connects the very small artery branches to very small veins. The capillaries have very thin walls, and through them, nutrients and oxygen are delivered to the cells.

Concept note-4: -How does oxygen get into the bloodstream? Inside the air sacs, oxygen moves across paper-thin walls to tiny blood vessels called capillaries and into your blood. A protein called haemoglobin in the red blood cells then carries the oxygen around your body.
