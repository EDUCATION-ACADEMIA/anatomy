Where does hematopoiesis take place, producing new RBCs?

(A) ** red bone marrow

(B) yellow bone marrow

(C) synovial membrane

(D) articular cartilage

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Where does hematopoiesis occur? The most common site of blood cell production is the spongy tissue inside of your bones called bone marrow. Hematopoiesis that occurs in your bone marrow is called medullary hematopoiesis. Blood cells get made in your bone marrow and released into your bloodstream.

Concept note-2: -After birth, and during early childhood, hematopoiesis occurs in the red marrow of the bone. With age, hematopoiesis becomes restricted to the skull, sternum, ribs, vertebrae, and pelvis. Yellow marrow, comprised of fat cells, replaces the red marrow and limits its potential for hematopoiesis.

Concept note-3: -Hematopoiesis produces new red blood cells in bone marrow. Specifically, hematopoiesis refers to the production of new blood cells and erythropoiesis refers to the production of new red blood cells. When red blood cells are decreased, the oxygen level also decreases in the body.

Concept note-5: -Hematopoiesis is responsible for the formation of all blood cells from hematopoietic stem cells (HSC) in the bone marrow (BM). It is a highly regulated process, in order to adapt its cellular output to changing body requirements.
