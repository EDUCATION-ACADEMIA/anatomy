Undifferentiated cells that have the potential to develop into any cell in the adult organism are called ____

(A) Primitive streak cells

(B) Carnegie cells

(C) ** Embryonic stem cells

(D) Trilaminar disk cells

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Pluripotent stem cells have the ability to differentiate into all of the cells of the adult body.

Concept note-2: -Adult stem cells, also called somatic stem cells, are undifferentiated cells that are found in many different tissues throughout the body of nearly all organisms, including humans.

Concept note-3: -Stem cells are different from other cells in the body in three ways: They can divide and renew themselves over a long time. They are unspecialized, so they cannot do specific functions in the body. They have the potential to become specialized cells, such as muscle cells, blood cells, and brain cells.

Concept note-4: -Stem cells are undifferentiated cells that can turn into specific cells, as the body needs them.

Concept note-5: -A stem cell is a cell with the unique ability to develop into specialised cell types in the body. In the future they may be used to replace cells and tissues that have been damaged or lost due to disease.
