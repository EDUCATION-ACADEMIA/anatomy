The dorsal portion of the inner ear consist of

(A) Cochlear duct and ductus reunions

(B) Saccule and cochlea

(C) Scala media

(D) ** Endolymphatic duct, utricle and saccule

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The inner ear is located within the bony labyrinth of the temporal bone and contains the cochlea, semicircular canals, utricle, and saccule. These organs make up the membranous labyrinth that is within the bony labyrinth, separated only by perilymph.

Concept note-2: -The endolymphatic duct originates in the saccule and runs inside an osseous duct, the vestibular aqueduct, until it reaches the subarachnoid space.

Concept note-3: -Your inner ear has three main parts: your cochlea, semi-circular canals (labyrinth) and your vestibule. Your cochlea supports your hearing and your vestibule and semi-circular canals support your balance.

Concept note-4: -the snail shaped organ for hearing (cochlea), the semicircular canals that help with balance, and. the nerves that go to the brain.
