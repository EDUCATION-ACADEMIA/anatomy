The opossum and kangaroo both incubate their young in a pouch. This provides evidence that they ____

(A) ** are descended from a common ancestor

(B) Have very similar skeletal structures

(C) belong to the same species

(D) must range great distances to eat

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The opossum, which is native to North America, and the kangaroo, which is native to Australia, are marsupials. The fact that both these mammals incubate their immature offspring in a pouch provides evidence that they-O are descended from a common ancestor.

Concept note-2: -Embryos of different species have differences and similarities in structures up to a certain period during gestation. Human, pig, reptile, and bird embryos have similar embryonic development but as they grow, they transform into their respective species. This demonstrates common ancestry.

Concept note-3: -Multiple types of evidence support the theory of evolution: Homologous structures provide evidence for common ancestry, while analogous structures show that similar selective pressures can produce similar adaptations (beneficial features).

Concept note-4: -Embryology is important to understanding a speciesâ€™ evolution, since some homologous structures can be seen only in embryo development. For example, all vertebrate embryos, from humans to chickens to fish, have a tail during early development, even if that tail does not appear in the fully developed organism.

Concept note-5: -Evidence of a common ancestor for all of life is reflected in the universality of DNA as the genetic material, in the near universality of the genetic code, and in the machinery of DNA replication and expression.
