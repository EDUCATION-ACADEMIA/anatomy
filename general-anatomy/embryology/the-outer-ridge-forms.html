The outer ridge forms

(A) Inner hair cells

(B) Outer hair cells

(C) ** Sensory hair cells

(D) None of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Sensory hair cells are specialized mechanosensory receptors found in vertebrate auditory, vestibular and lateral line organs that transduce vibratory and acoustic stimuli into the sensations of hearing and balance.

Concept note-2: -Hair cells are the sensory receptors of both the auditory system and the vestibular system in the ears of all vertebrates, and in the lateral line organ of fishes. Through mechanotransduction, hair cells detect movement in their environment.

Concept note-3: -Sensory hair cells are highly specialized mechanosensitive cells found in all vertebrate animals in some related chordates (tunicates). The structure of hair cells makes them highly sensitive to displacement of the fluid environment that surrounds their apical microvilli, or stereocilia.

Concept note-4: -Outer hair cells (OHCs) play an essential role in hearing by acting as a nonlinear amplifier which helps the cochlea detect sounds with high sensitivity and accuracy. This nonlinear sound processing generates distortion products, which can be measured as distortion-product otoacoustic emissions (DPOAEs).
