What would indicate that a series of fossils represent evolution?

(A) the fossils were identical

(B) the fossils were found in the same layers of rock

(C) ** the fossils show similarities

(D) the fossils were completely different

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Which would indicate that a series of fossils represent the evolution of the horse? All of the fossils were found in the same layers of rocks as horse fossils.

Concept note-2: -Fossils are the preserved remains or traces of animals, plants, and other organisms from the past. Fossils are important evidence for evolution because they show that life on earth was once different from life found on earth today.

Concept note-3: -Homologous features If two or more species share a unique physical feature, such as a complex bone structure or a body plan, they may all have inherited this feature from a common ancestor. Physical features shared due to evolutionary history (a common ancestor) are said to be homologous.

Concept note-4: -Anatomy and Embryology The similar construction of these appendages indicates that these organisms share a common ancestor. Another type of evidence for evolution is the presence of structures in organisms that share the same basic form.

Concept note-5: -Homologous structures provide evidence for common ancestry, while analogous structures show that similar selective pressures can produce similar adaptations (beneficial features).
