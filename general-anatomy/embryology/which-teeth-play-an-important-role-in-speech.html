Which teeth play an important role in speech?

(A) molars

(B) cuspids

(C) ** incisors

(D) premolars

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -When we say front teeth, we are referring to the four incisors, whether upper or lower. The middle two incisors are mainly used for making strident sounds such as ‘s’, ‘z’, ‘f’, ‘j’, ‘ch’, and ‘th’, so if those are missing, you will have a more pronounced speech impediment.

Concept note-2: -Incisors: These teeth are in the front of the mouth. They have a sharp biting surface and are sued for cutting food into small chewable pieces. Both children and adults have a total of 8 incisors.

Concept note-3: -Incisors are the teeth that we use to bite into our food. Canines-our canines are the next teeth that develop in your mouth. we have four of them and they are our sharpest teeth, used for tearing apart food. Premolars-Premolars are used for tearing and crushing food.

Concept note-4: -– The four front teeth in both the upper and lower jaws are called incisors. Their primary function is to cut food. The two incisors on either side of the midline are known as central incisors.

Concept note-5: -The answer is yes! The entire mouth is essential for speech, and this includes teeth. Any problem with your child’s teeth or oral development can affect their speech. The lips, tongue, and teeth all work together to form words by controlling the airflow out of the mouth.
