incontinence

(A) ** no control over excretory functions such as urination or feces

(B) inflammation of the bladder

(C) pus in the urine

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Bladder or bowel incontinence means a problem holding in urine or stool. You may have unwanted passage of urine or stool that you can’t control. These conditions can be stressful to deal with. But don’t feel embarrassed about talking to your healthcare provider.

Concept note-2: -There are four types of urinary incontinence: urgency, stress, functional and overflow incontinence. Behavioral therapies, medications, nerve stimulation and surgery are some of the treatments available for managing urinary incontinence.

Concept note-3: -Urinary incontinence means a person leaks urine by accident. While it can happen to anyone, urinary incontinence, also known as overactive bladder, is more common in older people, especially women.

Concept note-4: -Overview. Functional incontinence means that you can’t get to or use a toilet in time to urinate. This usually happens because something gets in your way or you aren’t able to walk there on your own. Functional incontinence usually occurs because something blocks your way to the toilet.
