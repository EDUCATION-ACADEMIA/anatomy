Which of the following is least likely to be present in the glomerular filtrate (the filtrate produced by the nephron before it enters the loop of Henle) of a healthy adult nephron?

(A) amino acids

(B) glucose

(C) electrolytes

(D) ** large protein molecules

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Large molecular weight protein are too large to pass through the glomerulus and into the tubular filtrate. So large molecular weight proteins are least likely to be present in the glomerular filtrate.

Concept note-2: -Plasma proteins have a higher molecular weight and is thus inhibited by the basement membrane of the glomerular capillaries from being filtered out. Hence, plasma protein is the least likely to be found in the glomerular filtrate.

Concept note-3: -Final answer: Glomerular filtrate does not contain proteins.

Concept note-4: -Correct answer: The correct path of filtrate through a nephron starts in the renal corpuscle, which is comprised of the glomerulus and Bowmanâ€™s capsule. Filtrate then passes through the proximal convoluted tubule, where the majority of reabsorption takes place.

Concept note-5: -The glomerular filtrate contains urea, water, vitamins, fatty acids, water, glucose, nitrogen, salts, etc. The glomerular filtrate enters the nephrons for further filtration termed ultrafiltration.
