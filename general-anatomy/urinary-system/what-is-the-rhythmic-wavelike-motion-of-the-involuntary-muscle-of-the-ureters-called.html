What is the rhythmic wavelike motion of the involuntary muscle of the ureters called?

(A) Urea

(B) Contraction

(C) ** Peristalsis

(D) Undulation

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Peristalsis is a series of wave-like muscle contractions that move food through the digestive tract. It starts in the esophagus where strong wave-like motions of the smooth muscle move balls of swallowed food to the stomach.

Concept note-2: -Ureteral peristalsis can be considered as a series of compressive zones, corresponding to waves of active muscular contraction, that move at near-constant speed along the ureter towards the bladder.

Concept note-3: -Peristalsis is a series of muscle contractions. These contractions occur in your digestive tract. Peristalsis is also seen in the tubes that connect the kidneys to the bladder. Peristalsis is an automatic and important process.

Concept note-4: -Urine is transported out of the papilla by a peristaltic process to the pelvis and ureters and then is stored in the bladder. Peristalsis is initiated in the renal pelvis and is propagated along the urinary tract by smooth muscle cells in the ureter coat.

Concept note-5: -What is the rhythmic wavelike motion of the involuntary muscle of the ureter called? What does it do? peristalsis; moves urine from the kidney to the bladder.
