Given these structures:Bowman’s capsule collecting duct distal tubule loop of Henle proximal tubule Choose the arrangement that lists the structures in order as filtrate leaves the glomerulus and travels to the renal calyx

(A) ** 1, 5, 4, 3, 2

(B) 4, 1, 2, 3, 5

(C) 1, 3, 4, 5, 2

(D) 2, 5, 3, 4, 1

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Correct answer: The correct path of filtrate through a nephron starts in the renal corpuscle, which is comprised of the glomerulus and Bowman’s capsule. Filtrate then passes through the proximal convoluted tubule, where the majority of reabsorption takes place.

Concept note-2: -Urine formation occurs in the kidney in three stages: filtration, reabsorption, and secretion.

Concept note-3: -The urine flows out of the nephron tubule into a collecting duct. It passes out of the kidney through the renal pelvis, into the ureter, and down to the bladder.

Concept note-4: -The correct answer is option (d) Proximal convoluted tubule, the loop of Henle, distal convoluted tubule, collecting duct. The renal tubule is a narrow tube connected to the renal corpuscle of a nephron where the processes of tubular reabsorption and tubular secretion occur.
