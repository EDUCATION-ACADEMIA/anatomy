GENERAL ANATOMY

URINARY SYSTEM

[SOURCES]
Kidneys are said to be retroperitoneal which means they are

(A) in front of the peritoneal cavity

(B) on the side of the peritoneal cavity

(C) ** behind the peritoneal cavity

(D) above the peritoneal cavity

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The kidneys are considered “retroperitoneal” organs, which means they sit behind a lining in the abdominal cavity, unlike all other abdominal organs.

Concept note-2: -The area in the back of the abdomen behind the peritoneum (the tissue that lines the abdominal wall and covers most of the organs in the abdomen). The organs in the retroperitoneum include the adrenal glands, aorta, kidneys, esophagus, ureters, pancreas, rectum, and parts of the stomach and colon.

Concept note-3: -The kidneys are called “retroperitoneal” organs, meaning that they reside behind a lining in the abdominal cavity, unlike all other abdominal organs.

Concept note-4: -Retroperitoneal organs lie behind the posterior sheath of the peritoneum and include the aorta, esophagus, second and third parts of the duodenum, ascending and descending colon, pancreas, kidneys, ureters, and adrenal glands.

Concept note-5: -Peritoneal Cavity: That part of the abdomen surrounded by peritoneum. It is a potential space between the parietal and visceral layers of peritoneum. Retroperitoneal Space: The area behind (posterior to) the peritoneum. Retroperitoneal organs are located in this space.
