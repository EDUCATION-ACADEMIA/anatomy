What is the urethra?

(A) tube that carries waste to the bladder

(B) ** tube that carries urine outside the body

(C) stores urine

(D) filters the blood

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The urine flows from the kidneys through the ureters to the bladder. The urine is stored in the bladder until it leaves the body through the urethra.

Concept note-2: -A urinary catheter is a flexible tube used to empty the bladder and collect urine in a drainage bag. Urinary catheters are usually inserted by a doctor or nurse.

Concept note-3: -(yoo-REE-thruh) The tube through which urine leaves the body. It empties urine from the bladder.

Concept note-4: -Answer and Explanation: The urethra carries urine from the bladder to the outside of the body. The urethra is responsible for carrying the urine which is stored in the urinary bladder to be transported out of the body. The urethra is the tube that carries the urine directly out of the body.

Concept note-5: -Ureters are long, narrow tubes that drain urine from your kidneys to your bladder. Normally one ureter leads from each kidney to your bladder. In the case of duplicated systems, two ureters drain a single kidney. One ureter drains the upper pole of your kidney and the other drains the lower pole.
