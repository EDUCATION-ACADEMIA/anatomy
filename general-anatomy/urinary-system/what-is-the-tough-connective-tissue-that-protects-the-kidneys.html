What is the tough connective tissue that protects the kidneys?

(A) Renal Cortex

(B) Renal Medulla

(C) Renal Pelvis

(D) ** Renal Fascia

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Each kidney is held in place by connective tissue, called renal fascia, and is surrounded by a thick layer of adipose tissue, called perirenal fat, which helps to protect it.

Concept note-2: -A fibrous envelope of tissue that surrounds the kidney. Also called Gerota’s capsule and Gerota’s fascia.

Concept note-3: -Gerota’s fascia (anterior renal fascia) is thin connective tissue (collagen) that surrounds your kidneys and adrenal glands. This tissue joins with posterior renal fascia to separate your kidneys from your other organs.

Concept note-4: -renal capsule, thin membranous sheath that covers the outer surface of each kidney. The capsule is composed of tough fibres, chiefly collagen and elastin (fibrous proteins), that help to support the kidney mass and protect the vital tissue from injury.

Concept note-5: -Anatomical reports have described the renal fascia as a membranous structure consisting of fibrous connective tissue covering the kidney and perirenal fat, as well as the ipsilateral ureter, the adrenal gland, and gonadal vessels from the anterior and posterior aspects in the retroperitoneum.
