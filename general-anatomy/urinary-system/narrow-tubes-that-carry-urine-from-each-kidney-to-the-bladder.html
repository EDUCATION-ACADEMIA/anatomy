Narrow tubes that carry urine from each kidney to the bladder

(A) ** Ureters

(B) Urine

(C) Bladder

(D) Urethra

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The tube that carries urine from the kidney to the bladder.

Concept note-2: -A urinary catheter is a flexible tube used to empty the bladder and collect urine in a drainage bag. Urinary catheters are usually inserted by a doctor or nurse.

Concept note-3: -The kidneys make urine. The ureters are tubes that carry urine from the kidneys to the bladder. The bladder stores urine. The urethra is a tube that carries urine from the bladder to the outside of the body.

Concept note-4: -Ureters are long, narrow tubes that drain urine from your kidneys to your bladder. Normally one ureter leads from each kidney to your bladder. In the case of duplicated systems, two ureters drain a single kidney. One ureter drains the upper pole of your kidney and the other drains the lower pole.

Concept note-5: -The ureter is a small tube, or duct, that connects the bladder and kidneys. Urine passes through the ureter from the kidneys to the bladder. The urethra is the tubular path that connects the bladder to the bodyâ€™s exterior, allowing urine to exit the body.
