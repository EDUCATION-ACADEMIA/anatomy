What does honey-colored urine indicate?

(A) ** You are dehydrated

(B) You are fine

(C) You are dying

(D) You ate too much honey

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Amber or honey colored urine may also mean you aren’t drinking enough water. Did you know some foods can change your urine color? For example, beets, blackberries and rhubarb can turn your urine red or pink. Eating large amounts of fava beans can cause dark brown urine.

Concept note-2: -Very dark honey-or brown-colored urine could be a sign that you’re dehydrated and need to get more fluids. It may also be a warning sign of liver problems, so see your doctor if it doesn’t get better after a day or so.

Concept note-3: -Medium-dark yellow urine is often an indication that you are dehydrated.

Concept note-4: -Share on Pinterest Brown urine is a symptom of dehydration. Dehydration occurs when the body lacks enough water to function properly. A person can become dehydrated for many reasons, including excessive sweating, urinating, and not drinking enough fluids. Darker or brown urine is a symptom of dehydration.

Concept note-5: -The relationship between urine colour and hydration status The issue is that, whilst urine colour can definitely be somewhat indicative of hydration status, there’s definitely not a simple and linear relationship between actual hydration status and the colour of your pee.
