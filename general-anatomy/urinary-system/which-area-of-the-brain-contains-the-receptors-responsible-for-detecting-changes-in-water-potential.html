Which area of the brain contains the receptors responsible for detecting changes in water potential

(A) ** Hypothalamus

(B) Medulla Oblongata

(C) Amygdala

(D) Hippocampus

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The primary osmoreceptors that control AVP secretion and thirst are located in the anterior hypothalamus.

Concept note-2: -The water concentration of the body is monitored by osmoreceptors in the hypothalamus, which detect the concentration of electrolytes in the extracellular fluid.

Concept note-3: -Extracellular dehydration is also detected by baroreceptors Baroreceptors respond to decreases or increases in blood pressure by inducing or inhibiting thirst, respectively, through their projections via cranial nerves IX and X to a brainstem region called the nucleus of the solitary tract.

Concept note-4: -Changes in osmolality are detected by specialized neurons called osmoreceptors in the anterior hypothalamus (Leng et al., 1985).
