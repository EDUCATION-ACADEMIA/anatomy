Where does urea exist before the kidney filters it out

(A) ** in bloodstream

(B) in intestines

(C) in liver

(D) in stomach

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The urea travels from your liver to your kidneys through your bloodstream. Healthy kidneys filter urea and remove other waste products from your blood.

Concept note-2: -Urea is carried in the bloodstream to the kidneys, where it is removed along with water and other wastes in the form of urine.

Concept note-3: -Urea is a natural product of nitrogen and protein metabolism and predominantly found in the urine and animal waste. This is the first organic compound artificially synthesized from inorganic starting materials.

Concept note-4: -Urine is formed in the kidneys through a filtration of blood. The urine is then passed through the ureters to the bladder, where it is stored. During urination, the urine is passed from the bladder through the urethra to the outside of the body.

Concept note-5: -The kidneys remove urea from the blood through tiny filtering units called nephrons. Each nephron consists of a ball formed of small blood capillaries (glomerulus) and a small tube called a renal tubule.
