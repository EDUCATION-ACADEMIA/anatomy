GENERAL ANATOMY

URINARY SYSTEM

[SOURCES]
Triangular shaped structures located in the renal medulla are called

(A) Renal columns

(B) Renal pelvises

(C) ** Pyramids

(D) Nephrons

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Triangular masses called renal pyramids are found in the renal medulla structure. Inside each renal pyramid, there are networks of nephrons.

Concept note-2: -The medulla is the inner region of the parenchyma of the kidney. The medulla consists of multiple pyramidal tissue masses, called the renal pyramids, which are triangle structures that contain a dense network of nephrons.

Concept note-3: -Renal pyramids are kidney tissues that are shaped like cones. Another term for renal pyramids is malpighian pyramids. Between seven and eighteen pyramids exist in the innermost part of the kidney, which is called the renal medulla; in humans, there are usually only seven of the pyramids.

Concept note-4: -The medulla is divided into 8-18 conical regions, called the renal pyramids; the base of each pyramid starts at the corticomedullary border, and the apex ends in the renal papilla which merges to form the renal pelvis and then on to form the ureter.

Concept note-5: -Definition. English. The medullary pyramids are paired white matter structures of the brainstem’s medulla oblongata that contain motor fibers of the corticospinal and corticobulbartracts – known together as the pyramidal tracts. The lower limit of the pyramids is marked when the fibers cross (decussate).
