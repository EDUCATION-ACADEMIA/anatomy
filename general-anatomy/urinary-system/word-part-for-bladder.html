Word part for bladder?

(A) ** cyst/o

(B) -cele

(C) glomerul/o

(D) nephr/o

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The combining form cysto-is used like a suffix meaning “cyst, ” which is a scientific term for a bladder, sac, or vesicle. It is often used in scientific terms, especially in biology and pathology. The form cysto-comes from Greek kýstis, meaning “bag, ” “pouch, ” or “bladder.” What are variants of cysto-?

Concept note-2: -cysto-in American English (ˈsɪstoʊ; ˈsɪstə ) bladder, sac. cystocele. Word origin. < Gr kystis, bladder, sac: see cyst.

Concept note-3: -cysto-, cyst-is a topic covered in the Taber’s Medical Dictionary. There’s more to see–the rest of this topic is available only to subscribers. Prefixes meaning the urinary bladder or a cyst.

Concept note-4: -(SIS-toh-SKOPE) A thin, tube-like instrument used to look inside the bladder and urethra. A cystoscope has a light and a lens for viewing and may have a tool to remove tissue.
