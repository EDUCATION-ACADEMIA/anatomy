What is urinary incontinence?

(A) ** inability to control release of urine

(B) inability to urinate

(C) painful urinary

(D) blood in the urine

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Overview. Urinary incontinence-the loss of bladder control-is a common and often embarrassing problem. The severity ranges from occasionally leaking urine when you cough or sneeze to having an urge to urinate that’s so sudden and strong you don’t get to a toilet in time.

Concept note-2: -Urinary incontinence means a person leaks urine by accident. While it can happen to anyone, urinary incontinence, also known as overactive bladder, is more common in older people, especially women. Bladder control issues can be embarrassing and cause people to avoid their normal activities.

Concept note-3: -Urinary incontinence is a loss of bladder control that’s commonly seen in older adults and women who have given birth or gone through menopause. Urinary tract infections (UTIs), pelvic floor disorders and an enlarged prostate are other causes. Outlook / Prognosis.

Concept note-4: -There are four types of urinary incontinence: urgency, stress, functional and overflow incontinence. Behavioral therapies, medications, nerve stimulation and surgery are some of the treatments available for managing urinary incontinence.

Concept note-5: -problems with passing urine, such as a slow stream of urine, straining to pass urine, or stopping and starting as you pass urine. problems after you’ve passed urine, such as feeling that you’ve not completely emptied your bladder or passing a few drops of urine after you think you’ve finished.
