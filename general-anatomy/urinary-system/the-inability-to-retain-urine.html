URINARY SYSTEM

DOWNLOAD FREE PDF BOOK (USEFUL FOR TEACHERS/INSTRUCTORS/COACHING INSTITUTES)

The inability to retain urine.

(A) Renal failure

(B) Dysuria

(C) Kidney stones

(D) ** Incontinence

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Urinary incontinence-the loss of bladder control-is a common and often embarrassing problem. The severity ranges from occasionally leaking urine when you cough or sneeze to having an urge to urinate that’s so sudden and strong you don’t get to a toilet in time.

Concept note-2: -This type of urinary incontinence causes a constant urine leak or dribble. Urinary retention due to a bladder obstruction, enlarged prostate or other problem can cause the bladder to get too full and overflow. Treatments include bladder training, medications and catheterization.

Concept note-3: -Urinary incontinence means a person leaks urine by accident. While it can happen to anyone, urinary incontinence, also known as overactive bladder, is more common in older people, especially women. Bladder control issues can be embarrassing and cause people to avoid their normal activities.

Concept note-4: -There are four types of urinary incontinence: urgency, stress, functional and overflow incontinence. Behavioral therapies, medications, nerve stimulation and surgery are some of the treatments available for managing urinary incontinence.

Concept note-5: -Urinary incontinence is a loss of bladder control that’s commonly seen in older adults and women who have given birth or gone through menopause. Urinary tract infections (UTIs), pelvic floor disorders and an enlarged prostate are other causes.
