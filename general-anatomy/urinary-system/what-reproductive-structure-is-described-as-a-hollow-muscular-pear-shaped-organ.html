What reproductive structure is described as a hollow, muscular, pear-shaped organ?

(A) ** Uterus

(B) Vagina

(C) Perineum

(D) Ovary

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The hollow, pear-shaped organ in a woman’s pelvis. The uterus is where a fetus (unborn baby) develops and grows. Also called womb.

Concept note-2: -The uterus is a hollow, pear-shaped organ in the lower abdomen. It is where a fetus grows during pregnancy.

Concept note-3: -Structure of the ovaries Each ovary is the same sort of shape and size as an almond, and the organs mature during puberty. The uterus is the hollow, pear-shaped organ where a fetus can grow.

Concept note-4: -The uterus, or womb, is a hollow, pear-shaped organ ln a woman’s lower stomach between the bladder and the rectum. It sheds its lining each month during menstruation. A fertilized egg (ovum) becomes implanted in the uterus, and the fetus develops. Vagina.

Concept note-5: -The uterus is a hollow, muscular, pear-shaped organ about the size of a woman’s clenched fist. The top is tipped forward in a normal ‘anteflexion’ position. It can be divided into the body or corpus, and the bottom cervix. The rounded top portion, above the fallopian tubes, is called the fundus.
