GENERAL ANATOMY

URINARY SYSTEM

[SOURCES]
notched or indented area in kidney through which the ureter, nerves, blood vessels, and lymph vessels enter and leave the kidney

(A) ** hilum

(B) ureters

(C) tubes

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Hilum of Kidney: Notched or indented area on each kidney. Area where ureter, nerves, blood vessels, and lymph vessels enter and leave the kidney.

Concept note-2: -The notch in the concave area of the kidney is called the hilum (HÄª lum). The renal artery, renal vein, nerves, and lymphatic vessels enter and leave through the hilum. The ureter also widens into a collection sac called the renal pelvis in the hilum area.

Concept note-3: -On the medial side of a kidney is an indentation called the hilus or hilum, at which the renal artery enters and the renal vein and ureter emerge.

Concept note-4: -The medial concave border of a kidney contains a notch known as hilus through which the renal artery enters and the renal vein and ureter leave the kidney.

Concept note-5: -The hilus is the notch near the center of the concave medial border through which the ureter leaves the kidney, and blood and lymphatic vessels and nerves enter and leave the kidney. The renal sinus is the cavity within the kidney that originates at the hilus.
