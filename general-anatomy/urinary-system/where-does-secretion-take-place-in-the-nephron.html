Where does secretion take place in the nephron?

(A) PCT

(B) Loop of Henle

(C) ** DCT

(D) Glomerulus

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -After reabsorption of K+ along the proximal tubule and loop of Henle, approximately 10% of filtered K+ reaches the DCT. As fluid travels down the DCT, the luminal potassium concentration increases, indicating that net K+ secretion occurs along the distal tubule.

Concept note-2: -This reabsorption occurs in the PCT, loop of Henle, DCT, and the collecting ducts while the majority of secretion occurs in the PCT and DCT (Table 25.5 and Figure 25.5.

Concept note-3: -The distal convoluted tubule also contains intercalated (I) cells, responsible for the secretion of acid. The collecting ducts are composed of I cells and also principal (P) cells, which become permeable to water under the influence of vasopressin.
