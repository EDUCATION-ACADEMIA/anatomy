Urine gets its normal yellow-amber or straw color from the pigment known as?

(A) bilirubin

(B) albumin

(C) ** urochrome

(D) hemoglobin

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Urine color generally ranges from a pale-yellow color to deep amber. This coloring is primarily caused by the pigment urochrome, also known as urobilin. Whether your urine is diluted by water or in a more concentrated form determines the appearance of the pigment.

Concept note-2: -Urobilin or urochrome is the chemical primarily responsible for the yellow color of urine. It is a linear tetrapyrrole compound that, along with the related colorless compound urobilinogen, are degradation products of the cyclic tetrapyrrole heme.

Concept note-3: -The most common color of urine is yellow, which is caused by the presence of urobilin, a biochemical waste product generated from the breakdown of old red blood cells. (Your body makes about 2 million new red blood cells every day, and recycles an equal number of old ones.)

Concept note-4: -component of human urine clear yellow from the pigment urochrome, an end product of protein metabolism. There are also traces of other pigments: urobilin and uroerythrin. The colour may be influenced as well by vitamins, food dyes, beetroot, and certain drugs.

Concept note-5: -Urobilin, also known as urochrome, is a tetrapyrroledicarboxylic acid that causes the yellow color in urine.
