GENERAL ANATOMY

URINARY SYSTEM

[SOURCES]
Which of the following is an example of the urinary system’s ability to maintain homeostasis?

(A) ** A person drinks a large amount of water and urinary output increases.

(B) The kidneys are protected by a heavy cushion of fat and a fibrous capsule.

(C) As people get older, the frequency of nocturne increases.

(D) When physical activity increases, the blood flow through the kidneys decreases.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The urinary system maintains blood homeostasis by filtering out excess fluid and other substances from the bloodstream and secreting waste.

Concept note-2: -The kidneys help regulate blood pressure through Na+ and water retention and loss. The kidneys work with the adrenal cortex, lungs, and liver in the renin–angiotensin–aldosterone system to regulate blood pressure. They regulate osmolarity of the blood by regulating both solutes and water.

Concept note-3: -Homeostasis is the body’s attempt to maintain a constant internal environment. One of the major ways the body achieves homeostasis is through excretion, the process of removing wastes and excess water from the body.

Concept note-4: -Sample answer: One way in which the excretory system helps maintain homeostasis in the body is by regulating water and salt balance through the function of the kidneys.
