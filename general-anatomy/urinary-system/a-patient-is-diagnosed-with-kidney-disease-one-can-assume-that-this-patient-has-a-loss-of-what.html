A patient is diagnosed with kidney disease. One can assume that this patient has a loss of what?

(A) The loop of Henle

(B) White blood cells

(C) Urine

(D) ** Nephrons

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The main test for kidney disease is a blood test. The test measures the levels of a waste product called creatinine in your blood. Your doctor uses your blood test results, plus your age, size, gender and ethnic group to calculate how many millilitres of waste your kidneys should be able to filter in a minute.

Concept note-2: -Chronic kidney disease (CKD) is the slow, gradual loss of kidney function.

Concept note-3: -Acute kidney failure occurs when your kidneys suddenly become unable to filter waste products from your blood. When your kidneys lose their filtering ability, dangerous levels of wastes may accumulate, and your bloodâ€™s chemical makeup may get out of balance.

Concept note-4: -A typical kidney has about 1 million filtering units. Each unit, called a glomerulus, connects to a tubule, which collects urine. Conditions such as high blood pressure and diabetes take a toll on kidney function by damaging these filtering units and collecting tubules and causing scarring.
