Which division of the nervous system is responsible for the rest & digest response?

(A) Afferent

(B) Efferent

(C) ** Parasympathetic

(D) Sympathetic

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Your parasympathetic nervous system’s job is usually to relax or reduce your body’s activities. Because of the signals it carries, the rhyming phrases “rest and digest” or “feed and breed” are easy ways to remember what your parasympathetic nervous system does.

Concept note-2: -The parasympathetic nervous system, also known as “rest and digest, ” can be thought of as functioning in opposition to the sympathetic nervous system. Its functions include: A decrease in heart rate and contractility of cardiac muscle. Constriction of the ciliary muscle and the pupil for near vision.

Concept note-3: -The parasympathetic nervous system is responsible for the body’s rest and digestion response when the body is relaxed, resting, or feeding. It basically undoes the work of sympathetic division after a stressful situation. The parasympathetic nervous system decreases respiration and heart rate and increases digestion.

Concept note-4: -It is called the “rest and digest” division of the ANS. The parasympathetic nervous system decreases respiration and heart rate and increases digestion.

Concept note-5: -Examples of parasympathetic responses This stands for: Salivation: As part of its rest-and-digest function, the PSNS stimulates production of saliva, which contains enzymes to help your food digest.
