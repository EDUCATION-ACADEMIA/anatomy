narrow gaps between Schwann cells

(A) neurilemma

(B) neurofibrils

(C) ** nodes of Ranvier

(D) atrocytes

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In myelinated axons, Schwann cells form the myelin sheath. The sheath is not continuous. Individual myelinating Schwann cells cover about 1 mm of an axon-equating to about 1000 Schwann cells along a 1-m length of the axon. The gaps between adjacent Schwann cells are called nodes of Ranvier.
