Which way does an impulse travel

(A) ** From dendrite to axon and out axon terminal

(B) From axon to axon and out dendrite terminal

(C) From axon to dendrite and out axon terminal

(D) From cell body to dendrite and out axon terminal

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Final answer: The electrical impulse travels in a neuron from Dendrite → cell body → axon → axonal end.

Concept note-2: -Nerve impulses begin in a dendrite, move toward the cell body, and then move down the axon. A nerve impulse travels along the neuron in the form of electrical and chemical signals. The axon tip ends at a synapse. A synapse is the junction between each axon tip and the next structure.

Concept note-3: -The impulse always travels from one dendron end of a neuron to another, from one axon to another.

Concept note-4: -Impulse enters a neuron through the dendrites. The information acquired at the end of the dendritic tip of a nerve cell sets off a chemical reaction that sends the impulse to the cell body where it is processed. From the cell body, the impulse moves into the axon and then to the axonal ends.

Concept note-5: -Neurotransmitters travel across the synapse between the axon and the dendrite of the next neuron. Neurotransmitters bind to the membrane of the dendrite. The binding allows the nerve impulse to travel through the receiving neuron.
