The part of the brain which controls voluntary actions

(A) ** Cerebellum

(B) Cerebrum

(C) Medulla oblongata

(D) Pons

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The cerebellum receives information from the sensory systems, the spinal cord, and other parts of the brain and then regulates control of movements. The cerebellum controls voluntary movements such as: walking. posture.

Concept note-2: -All the voluntary actions of our body are controlled by the cerebrum. The cerebrum is the largest and the most prominent part of the brain.

Concept note-3: -Cerebellum. This is the back of the brain. It coordinates voluntary muscle movements and helps to maintain posture, balance, and equilibrium.

Concept note-4: -The cerebellum, which means “little brain, ” is a part of the brain involved in coordinating movement and balance. It can also play a role in cognitive functions like language and attention.
