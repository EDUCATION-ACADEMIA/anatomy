How many times do you need to see the eye doctor a year?

(A) 2 times

(B) ** 1 time

(C) 10 times

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Most eye care professionals advise their patients to have a comprehensive eye exam every year.

Concept note-2: -If you don’t have any symptoms or vision problems, doctors recommend getting regular eye exams based on your age: Ages 20 to 39: Every 5 years. Ages 40 to 54: Every 2 to 4 years. Ages 55 to 64: Every 1 to 3 years.

Concept note-3: -It’s recommended that most people should get their eyes tested every 2 years.

Concept note-4: -Our optometrists and ophthalmologists recommend: If you are younger than 40, you should have an eye test every 2 years, regardless of whether or not you have a visual impairment such as myopia or hyperopia. If you are 40 or older, you should have your eyes tested once a year.

Concept note-5: -Proper eye examination, timely identification and lens replacement surgery is very essential to restore normal vision. Full eye check-up also has the ability to identify age-related macular degeneration, which could lead to sight loss. Properly scheduled eye exams can detect several serious health problems.
