Sensing hot or cold

(A) Central Nervous System (CNS)

(B) ** Peripheral Nervous System (PNS)

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Temperature information is sensed by neurons with cell bodies in primary sensory ganglia (or trigeminal ganglia), and then transmitted to the dorsal horn of the spinal cord (or chief sensory nucleus of V), the lateral parabrachial nuclei and finally the preoptic area.

Concept note-2: -Your PNS feeds information into your brain from most of your senses. It carries signals that allow you to move your muscles. Your PNS also delivers signals that your brain uses to control vital, unconscious processes like your heartbeat and breathing.

Concept note-3: -Free nerve endings are considered to be the somatosensory receptors for pain, temperature and crude touch. Proprioceptors are located in muscles, tendons, joint ligaments and in joint capsules.

Concept note-4: -Sensory neurons are the afferent limb of somatosensory neural pathways. The neuron consists of a cell body, axon, and dendrites. Dendrites are finger-like projections that receive sensory input and transmit the signal through the axon to the cell body.
