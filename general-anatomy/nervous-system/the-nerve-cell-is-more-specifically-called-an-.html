The nerve cell is more specifically called a(n) ____

(A) axon

(B) dendrite

(C) myelin sheath

(D) ** neuron

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Neurons (also called neurones or nerve cells) are the fundamental units of the brain and nervous system, the cells responsible for receiving sensory input from the external world, for sending motor commands to our muscles, and for transforming and relaying the electrical signals at every step in between.

Concept note-2: -A type of cell that receives and sends messages from the body to the brain and back to the body. The messages are sent by a weak electrical current. Also called neuron.

Concept note-3: -Nerve cells are also called neurons. Neurons are present all over your body, especially in your brain and spinal cord. Nerves, together with your brain and spinal cord, are the foundation of your nervous system.

Concept note-4: -Another name for a nerve cell is a neuron.

Concept note-5: -Motor neurons have the most common type of ‘body plan’ for a nerve cell-they are multipolar, each with one axon and several dendrites.
