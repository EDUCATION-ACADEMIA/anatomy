Optic Nerve

(A) Cranial nerve I

(B) ** Cranial nerve II

(C) Cranial nerve IX

(D) Cranial nerve XII

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The optic nerve is the second cranial nerve (CN II) responsible for transmitting visual information. The optic nerve contains only afferent (sensory) fibers, and like all cranial nerves is paired.

Concept note-2: -Neuroanatomy, Cranial Nerve 2 (Optic)

Concept note-3: -The optic nerve is the second of 12 cranial nerves. Each eye has its own optic nerve. The optic nerve: Starts in the optic disk, a group of cells in retinal tissue at the back of your eye.

Concept note-4: -Cranial nerves I (olfactory), II (optic), and VIII (vestibulocochlear) are considered purely afferent.

Concept note-5: -The optic nerve travels through the optic canal, partially decussates in the optic chiasm, and terminates in the lateral geniculate nucleus where information is transmitted to the visual cortex.
