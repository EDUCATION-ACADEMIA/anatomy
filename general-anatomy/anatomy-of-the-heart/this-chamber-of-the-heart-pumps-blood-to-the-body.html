This chamber of the heart PUMPS blood to the body.

(A) Right Atrium

(B) Right Ventricle

(C) Left Atrium

(D) ** Left Ventricle

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The left atrium receives oxygen-rich blood from the lungs and pumps it through the mitral valve to the left ventricle. The left ventricle pumps the oxygen-rich blood through the aortic valve out to the rest of the body.

Concept note-2: -The oxygen-rich blood from the lungs then enters the left atrium and is pumped to the left ventricle. The left ventricle generates the high pressure needed to pump the blood to your whole body through your blood vessels.

Concept note-3: -The left ventricle is the main pumping chamber of the heart and pumps blood to the body. The right ventricle pumps blood to the lungs to pick up oxygen.

Concept note-4: -Normally, the left ventricle, one of your heartâ€™s four chambers, pumps blood into your aorta (the large artery leaving the heart) and around your body.
