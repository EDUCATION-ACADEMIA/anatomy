GENERAL ANATOMY

ANATOMY OF THE HEART

[SOURCES]
The longest vein in the body is the ____

(A) inferior vena cava

(B) femoral vein

(C) ** great saphenous vein

(D) ulnar vein

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Your great saphenous vein is also called your long saphenous vein, and it’s the longest vein in your body, traveling between your foot and the top of your thigh.

Concept note-2: -Did you know that your Great Saphenous Vein is the longest vein in the human body? Extending from the top of your foot to the upper thigh and groin, THIS vein is the major culprit that causes Varicose Veins.

Concept note-3: -Your inferior vena cava, your body’s largest vein, carries oxygen-depleted blood back to your heart from the lower part of your body (below your diaphragm).

Concept note-4: -Out of these two veins of the heart, the inferior vena cava (IVC) is considered as the longest of all the veins found in the human body.

Concept note-5: -The aorta, which connects the heart’s left ventricle to the main high-pressure pipeline, is the biggest artery. The blood channels that transport oxygen-rich blood from the heart to the body’s tissues are known as arteries. More items
