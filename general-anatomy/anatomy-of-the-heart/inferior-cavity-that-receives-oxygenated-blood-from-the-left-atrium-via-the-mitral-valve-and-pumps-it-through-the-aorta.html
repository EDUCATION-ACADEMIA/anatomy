Inferior cavity that receives oxygenated blood from the left atrium via the mitral valve and pumps it through the aorta

(A) Right Atrium

(B) Left Atrium

(C) Right Ventricle

(D) ** Left Ventricle

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The left ventricle (LV) pumps the oxygen-rich blood through the aortic valve (AoV) into the aorta (Ao), the main artery that takes oxygen-rich blood out to the rest of the body.

Concept note-2: -Right atrium Blood enters through two large veins called your superior vena cava and your inferior vena cava. Your right atrium takes this blood and passes it on to the next player, your right ventricle.

Concept note-3: -The posterior descending artery branch supplies blood to the inferior aspect of the heart. The LMCA supplies blood to the left side of the heart. The LAD provides blood to the anterior ventricular septum and the greater portion of the anterior portion of the left ventricle.

Concept note-4: -The oxygenated blood is brought back to the heart by the pulmonary veins which enter the left atrium. From the left atrium blood flows into the left ventricle.
