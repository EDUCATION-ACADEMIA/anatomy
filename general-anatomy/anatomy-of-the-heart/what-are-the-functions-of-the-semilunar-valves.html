What are the functions of the semilunar valves?

(A) ** prevent blood from leaking back into the ventricles

(B) prevent blood from leaking back into the atria

(C) allow blood to flow into the atria

(D) allow blood to leave the ventricles

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The semilunar valves permit blood to flow into the arteries from the ventricles and prevent the backward flow of blood from the arteries into the ventricles. The semilunar valves are made up of connective tissue and a thin smooth membrane known as the endocardium.

Concept note-2: -The valve between the left ventricle and the aorta is the aortic semilunar valve. When the ventricles contract, atrioventricular valves close to prevent blood from flowing back into the atria. When the ventricles relax, semilunar valves close to prevent blood from flowing back into the ventricles.

Concept note-3: -The semilunar valves determine the passage of blood between the ventricles and the main arteries, transporting blood away from the heart to the vital organs.

Concept note-4: -What is the function of a semilunar valve? The function of a semilunar valve is to carry the blood to the distant parts of the body. It also prevents the flow of blood from the aorta and pulmonary artery to the ventricles of the heart.

Concept note-5: -The atrioventricular valves prevent backflow of blood into the atria during ventricular contraction (systole), and the aortic/pulmonic (semilunar) valves prevent backflow of blood into the ventricles during ventricular relaxation (diastole).
