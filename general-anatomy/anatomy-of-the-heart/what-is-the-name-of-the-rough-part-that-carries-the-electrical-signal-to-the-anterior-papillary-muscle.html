What is the name of the rough part that carries the electrical signal to the anterior papillary muscle?

(A) Pectoral muscles

(B) ** Moderator band

(C) Classical trabecular caneae

(D) Tendon chords

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The moderator band (MB, also known as the septomarginal trabecula) found in all human hearts is derived from the muscle band of the interventricular septum, begins below the septal end of the supraventricular crest, and runs toward the anterolateral wall of the ventricle [1].

Concept note-2: -They arise from the inner walls of the left and right ventricle and attach to mitral and tricuspid valve leaflets respectively via chordae tendinae. Historical documentation of the existence of papillary muscles as a component of cardiac anatomy exists at least as early as the 16th century.

Concept note-4: -Its main function is to convey the right branch of the atrioventricular bundle of the conducting system. The septomarginal trabecula forms the anteroinferior border between the superior, smooth outflow tract of the ventricle and the trabeculated inflow tract.

Concept note-5: -Blood supply typically originates from septal perforating branches of the left anterior descending artery (LAD) which may subsequently anastomose with vessels derived from the right coronary circulation.
