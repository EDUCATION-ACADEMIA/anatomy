Which valve is also called the mitral valve and prevents flow back into the left atrium from the left ventricle and is an AV valve?

(A) aortic

(B) ** bicuspid

(C) pulmonary

(D) tricuspid

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Mitral valve: This valve has two leaflets. They allow blood to flow from the lungs into the left atrium. And they prevent backward flow from the left ventricle to the left atrium. Aortic valve: This valve has three leaflets.

Concept note-2: -Mitral Valve Separates the top left chamber (left atrium) from the bottom left chamber (left ventricle). Opens to allow blood to flow from the left atrium to the left ventricle. Prevents the back flow of blood from the left ventricle to the left atrium.

Concept note-3: -The mitral valve is also known as the bicuspid valve. This is one of the heart’s four valves that help prevent blood from flowing backward as it moves through the heart. Read on to learn more about the mitral valve, including its location and anatomy.

Concept note-4: -The mitral valve is also called the bicuspid valve because it contains two leaflets or cusps.

Concept note-5: -The mitral valve (/ˈmaɪtrəl/), also known as the bicuspid valve or left atrioventricular valve, is one of the four heart valves. It has two cusps or flaps and lies between the left atrium and the left ventricle of the heart. The heart valves are all one-way valves allowing blood flow in just one direction.
