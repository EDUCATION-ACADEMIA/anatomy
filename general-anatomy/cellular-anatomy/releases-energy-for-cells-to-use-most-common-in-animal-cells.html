CELLULAR ANATOMY

[SOURCES]
releases energy for cells to use, most common in animal cells

(A) ** mitochondria

(B) ribosomes

(C) lysosomes

(D) nucleolus

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Mitochondria are membrane-bound cell organelles (mitochondrion, singular) that generate most of the chemical energy needed to power the cell’s biochemical reactions. Chemical energy produced by the mitochondria is stored in a small molecule called adenosine triphosphate (ATP).

Concept note-2: -In fact, most of the energy used by the cells in your body is provided by cellular respiration. Just as photosynthesis occurs in organelles called chloroplasts, cellular respiration takes place in organelles called mitochondria.

Concept note-3: -Mitochondria are organelles – ‘small organs’ within each cell. They produce energy in the form of a molecule called ATP (adenosine triphosphate) which gets used throughout the cell to power the different jobs it has to do.

Concept note-4: -Like animal cells, plant cells also have mitochondria. The mitochondria play a critical role in integrating the sugar that is produced in the cell to generate energy. Mitochondria are necessary for plants to produce energy in ATP form, which is normally done during the day through photosynthesis.

Concept note-5: -The process of ATP synthesis and cellular respiration takes place inside the mitochondria. Hence, mitochondria are the cell organelle that releases the most energy.
