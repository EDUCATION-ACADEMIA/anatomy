What does it mean to be multicellular?

(A) Any living thing

(B) ** Organisms that consist of many cells

(C) Organisms that consist of a single cell

(D) The basic unit of life

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Multicellular organisms are made of more than one cell and are complex organisms. They are visible to the naked eye. They possess distinct organs and organ systems. They are eukaryotes, i.e., they contain membrane-bound structures. Their cells exhibit division of labour.

Concept note-2: -Multicellular organisms are made up of multiple cells instead of one large cell. This is so because, as the cell size increases the surface area and the volume increases.

Concept note-3: -Multicellular organisms are composed of more than one cell, with groups of cells differentiating to take on specialized functions. In humans, cells differentiate early in development to become nerve cells, skin cells, muscle cells, blood cells, and other types of cells.

Concept note-4: -Multicellular organisms are made up of many cells. The cells in multicellular organisms are often specialized to carry out specific functions. All cells come from other cells. New cells are made through a process called cell division.

Concept note-5: -Organisms which are made up of more than one cell are known as multicellular. Multicellular organisms are said to be complex in nature. As they are made up of many different cells. An example of multicellular organism would be human beings.
