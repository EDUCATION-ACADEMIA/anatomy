Small vesicles that digest materials in the cell using enzymes.

(A) Ribosomes

(B) ** Lysosomes

(C) Nucleus

(D) Mitochondria

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Lysosomes are membrane-enclosed organelles that contain an array of enzymes capable of breaking down all types of biological polymers-proteins, nucleic acids, carbohydrates, and lipids.

Concept note-2: -A lysosome is a membrane-bound cell organelle that contains digestive enzymes.

Concept note-3: -Lysosomes are vesicles that contain digestive enzymes. They are only present in animal cells. They function as part of the cellâ€™s recycling system and can also help initiate cell death.

Concept note-4: -Lysosomes are small vesicles derived from the Golgi apparatus; they contain up to 40 acidic enzymes (hydrolases) at a pH 5. The membrane prevents the aggressive enzymes from destroying cellular structures.

Concept note-5: -Enzymes of the lysosomes are synthesised in the rough endoplasmic reticulum. The enzymes are imported from the Golgi apparatus in small vesicles, which fuse with larger acidic vesicles.
