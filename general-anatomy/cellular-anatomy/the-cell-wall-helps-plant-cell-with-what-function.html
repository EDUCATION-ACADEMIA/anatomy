The cell wall helps plant cell with what function?

(A) It is where genetic information is found.

(B) ** It adds an extra layer of protection of the cell, and helps give it structure and shape.

(C) It is another area where energy is created for the cell, besides the mitochondria.

(D) It takes in energy from the sun to assist in photosynthesis.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The main function of the cell wall is to provide structural strength and support, and also provide a semi-permeable surface for molecules to pass in and out of the cell.

Concept note-2: -The cell wall surrounds the plasma membrane of plant cells and provides tensile strength and protection against mechanical and osmotic stress. It also allows cells to develop turgor pressure, which is the pressure of the cell contents against the cell wall.

Concept note-3: -Plant cells are surrounded by a tough extracellular matrix in the form of a cell wall, which is responsible for many of the unique features of a plantâ€™s life style.

Concept note-4: -The cell is surrounded by a plasma membrane (or cell membrane) to separate the cell contents from the external environment. Biology definition: A cell wall is a structural layer next to the cell membrane with the primary role of giving the cell rigidity and protection from mechanical stress.

Concept note-5: -As plants cannot move from one place to another so they need greater protection against variations in temperature, high wind speed, atmospheric moisture, etc. Therefore, for protection they have an additional layer surrounding the cell membrane This layer is called the cell wall.
