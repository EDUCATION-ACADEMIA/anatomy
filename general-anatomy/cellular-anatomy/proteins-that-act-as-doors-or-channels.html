proteins that act as “doors” or channels

(A) peripheral

(B) fibrous

(C) ** globular

(D) C.A.M.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Some globular proteins found in cell membranes act as transport channels for ions and other molecules.

Concept note-2: -In biochemistry, globular proteins or spheroproteins are spherical ("globe-like") proteins and are one of the common protein types (the others being fibrous, disordered and membrane proteins). Globular proteins are somewhat water-soluble (forming colloids in water), unlike the fibrous or membrane proteins.

Concept note-3: -Spherical proteins are also known as globular proteins or hemoproteins. They are proteins that are spherical in shape. Fibrous, disordered, and membrane proteins are the other common protein shapes. Globular proteins, unlike fibrous or membrane proteins, are generally water-soluble (forming colloids in water).

Concept note-4: -Globular proteins are categorized into four structural classes: all-, all-, +, and / (Levitt and Chothia, 1976).
