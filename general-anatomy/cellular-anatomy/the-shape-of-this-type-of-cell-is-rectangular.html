The shape of this type of cell is rectangular.

(A) ** plant cell

(B) animal cell

(C) bacteria cell

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Plant cells have a cell wall, as well as a cell membrane. In plants, the cell wall surrounds the cell membrane. This gives the plant cell its unique rectangular shape.

Concept note-2: -Plant cells are rectangular shaped, eukaryotic, or nucleus-containing, cells with a rigid cell wall. Within the plant cell structures called organelles, perform functions necessary to cell and plant survival.

Concept note-3: -Often represented in 2D (like in your textbook) plant cells appear rectangular. In actuality, they are mostly rectangular cubes! Plant cells, unlike animal cells, have a cell wall that is made of cellulose and gives them a cuboidal as opposed to spherical structure.

Concept note-4: -The main difference between an animal cell and a plant cell is that plant cells have a cell wall and animal cell lacks the cell wall. The cell wall which is made up of cellulose gives the plant cell rigidity resulting in a fixed, rectangular shape.
