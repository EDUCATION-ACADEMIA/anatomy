Jelly-like substance that fills space between organelles. The “roads” of the cell.

(A) Chloroplast

(B) Ribosome

(C) ** Cytoplasm

(D) Nucleus

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Cytoplasm: Cytoplasm is the jelly-like and semi-fluid structure present in the space between the cell membrane and nucleus. Cytoplasm provides turgidity to the cell.

Concept note-2: -Cytoplasm Cytoplasm is the gelatinous liquid that fills the inside of a cell. It is composed of water, salts, and various organic molecules. Some intracellular organelles, such the nucleus and mitochondria, are enclosed by membranes that separate them from the cytoplasm.

Concept note-3: -The cytoplasm, or cell fluid, is made up of a jelly-like substance (cytosol) and within that, the organelles. The organelles are the cell’s organs. The cytoplasm is often colourless and is surrounded by the cell membrane which keeps the contents within the cell.

Concept note-4: -The jelly-like fluid present between the cell membrane and the nuclear membrane is called the cytoplasm.

Concept note-5: -Cytoplasm. Explanation : The cytoplasm is a thick jelly-like substance which fills most of the cells inside and is enclosed by the cell membrane.
