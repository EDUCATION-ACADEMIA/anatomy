hold/store water, mainly found in plant cells

(A) nuclolus

(B) ribosomes

(C) golgi bodies

(D) ** vacuoles

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The central vacuole is a large vacuole found inside of plant cells. A vacuole is a sphere filled with fluid and molecules inside a cell. The central vacuole stores water and maintains turgor pressure in a plant cell.

Concept note-2: -In a plant cell, water is stored in sacs called vacuoles. The cell becomes rigid and firm. When the sacs are completely filled with, vacuoles moves on to the cell wall and cell membrane, then the cell becomes turgid.

Concept note-3: -Vacuoles. First, plant cells have a large central vacuole that holds a mixture of water, nutrients, and wastes. A plant cell’s vacuole can make up 90% of the cell’s volume. The large central vacuole essentially stores water.

Concept note-4: -Among its roles in plant cell function, the central vacuole stores salts, minerals, nutrients, proteins, pigments, helps in plant growth, and plays an important structural role for the plant.
