Intercellular junctions that form “spot welds” between cells.

(A) tight junctions

(B) ** desmosomes

(C) gap junctions

(D) plasmodesmata

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Desmosomes. Animal cells may also contain junctions called desmosomes, which act like spot welds between adjacent epithelial cells. A desmosome involves a complex of proteins. Some of these proteins extend across the membrane, while others anchor the junction within the cell.

Concept note-2: -Desmosomes are intercellular junctions that provide strong adhesion between cells. Because they also link intracellularly to the intermediate filament cytoskeleton they form the adhesive bonds in a network that gives mechanical strength to tissues.

Concept note-3: -Desmosomes form links between cells, and provide a connection between intermediate filaments of the cell cytoskeletons of adjacent cells. This structure gives strength to tissues. Finally, the need for signaling is a function of gap junctions that form pores connecting adjacent cells.

Concept note-4: -In Summary: Cell Junctions Plasmodesmata are channels between adjacent plant cells, while gap junctions are channels between adjacent animal cells. However, their structures are quite different. A tight junction is a watertight seal between two adjacent cells, while a desmosome acts like a spot weld.

Concept note-5: -Desmosomes are one of the stronger cell-to-cell adhesion types and are found in tissue that experience intense mechanical stress, such as cardiac muscle tissue, bladder tissue, gastrointestinal mucosa, and epithelia.
